**George MacDonald believed that the greatest question we can ask is this: *What is God really like?***

For more than 150 years, *Unspoken Sermons* has helped readers discover an answer that is both deeply biblical and profoundly hopeful. Rather than portraying God as distant or severe, MacDonald reveals the Father made known in Jesus Christ—a God whose holiness is perfect love, whose judgment restores, and whose desire is to bring His children into the fullness of life.

A Scottish minister, novelist, and one of the most influential Christian writers of the nineteenth century, George MacDonald inspired generations of believers, including C. S. Lewis, G. K. Chesterton, and Madeleine L'Engle. His vision of God's goodness continues to speak with remarkable freshness today.

This devotional adaptation faithfully renders the **First Series of *Unspoken Sermons*** into clear, contemporary English while preserving MacDonald's voice and theological heart. Each reading begins with Scripture, unfolds a vision of God's character, offers quiet reflection, and concludes with a brief breath prayer.

Inside you'll explore themes such as God's fatherly love, Christ as the perfect revelation of the Father, the refining work of divine love, childlike faith, forgiveness, restoration, resurrection, and the hope of all things made new.

Whether you are discovering George MacDonald for the first time or returning to a beloved teacher, these devotions invite you to slow down, behold the Father's heart, and discover that His love is deeper, wiser, and more beautiful than you ever imagined.
