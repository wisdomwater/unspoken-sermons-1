## 47 - Love Never Prays Alone

> *Love cannot remain silent before another's suffering. Every prayer born of love is already a participation in the Father's own heart.*

If the Father already knows every need and loves every soul more deeply than we ever could, why should we pray for one another? It is a question that humbles the mind. Yet the heart refuses to remain silent. When love awakens within us, it longs to bless, to heal, to lift another toward life. Finding itself unable to do what it desires, it naturally turns to the One who can. Prayer is not an attempt to persuade God to love; it is love itself seeking its source and asking to become His companion.

The Father has not created His children merely to receive His goodness, but to share in it. He delights to make us fellow workers in His redeeming love. Just as a child longs to bring a gift to a brother yet has nothing to give except what the father first places in his hands, so we come before God asking that His love may flow through us for another. Every prayer offered for a friend, an enemy, or a stranger becomes another way of saying, "Father, let me share Your heart."

How this mystery unfolds we cannot fully explain. Yet love itself gives us reason to hope. If we live in God, then we are nearer to one another than we can ever measure. No distance, no silence, not even death can finally separate those who are held within the life of the Eternal. A prayer offered in love is never lost. It passes into the boundless love of God, who knows how to weave it into His perfect work in ways beyond our seeing.

Nor should we lose heart if the answer first appears as a deeper trial. The Father often heals by a path we would never have chosen. Sometimes He answers our prayer for love by giving us someone difficult to forgive. Sometimes He answers our prayer for humility by uncovering our pride. His answers always serve the life He is forming within us. Therefore let your hope be larger than your understanding. The Father's wisdom is wider than your sight, and His love is already accomplishing more than you can yet imagine.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your love flow through my prayers for others.*
