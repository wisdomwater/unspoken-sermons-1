## 85 - The Kingdom of the True

> *Christ is King because nothing in Him is false. His throne is truth, His crown is love, and His kingdom is formed wherever a human heart becomes true.*

When Pilate asked Jesus whether He was a king, he imagined the kingdoms of this world—armies, authority, rank, command. But Jesus had no hunger for such power. He could have gathered nations beneath His rule, yet He would rather awaken one human heart to love its neighbor. He would rather wash the feet of weary friends than sit upon the greatest throne ever raised. For His kingdom is not built by making others small, but by making them true.

The kingdom of Christ is a kingdom of kings. He does not desire subjects who crouch beneath Him, but sons and daughters who rise into the dignity for which the Father created them. In His kingdom no one seeks to stand above another. There is no ambition, no grasping for praise, no need to gather the eyes of others toward oneself. Christ reigns wherever a person becomes faithful to God, forgetful of self, and loving toward every brother and sister.

Jesus is King because His entire life bears witness to the Father. Every thought, word, and deed declares that God is good. His freedom lies in having no will apart from the Father’s, not because His own will has been crushed, but because it has reached perfection in love. He does not obey a stranger. He shares the heart of the One from whom He comes. In Jesus we see that surrender to God is not the loss of the self, but the flowering of the true self.

The cross is therefore His highest throne. Mocked as a king, clothed in ridicule, and crowned with pain, He remains true. Men may call His witness a lie, but He will not cease to bear it. They may kill Him, but they cannot make Him false. Even beneath cruelty, He continues to reveal the Father’s love. Never was Christ more kingly than when He refused to save Himself by betraying the truth He came to show.

You too are called to bear witness—not chiefly through argument, but through the manner of your life. Truth is not the same as opinion, and faithfulness does not require you to speak at every moment. Yet you must not live as though what is true does not matter. In your home, your work, your speech, your silence, your treatment of the overlooked, let your life take the side of Christ. The kingdom comes wherever someone would rather lose honor than become false, and would rather serve in love than rule through fear.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Jesus, make my life a witness to the Father.*
