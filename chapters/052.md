## 52 - Abba, Father

> *The deepest cry of the human heart is not to be accepted as a stranger, but to awaken as a beloved child. We do not long for adoption because we have lost a Father—we long for the Father because we have always belonged to Him.*

There is no sweeter word than **Father** when it rises freely from the heart. It is also one of the hardest words to speak with full confidence, for many have been taught to imagine God as One who first stands at a distance, deciding whether He will receive them. Yet Christ did not come to persuade a reluctant God to become our Father. He came to reveal the Father who has loved His children from the beginning.

Something within us recoils at the thought that God might only become our Father by some legal arrangement. Love knows better. A father is not made by decree but by life itself. The One who called you into being, who even now upholds your every breath, cannot be merely your Maker while remaining a stranger to your heart. You bear His image, however scarred it has become. That image may be hidden beneath sin, fear, and pride, but it is still His image, and it is because you belong to Him that your soul cannot rest apart from Him.

Indeed, your very grief over your distance from God is evidence of your true home. Why should your heart ache to call Him Father if there were no such relationship to awaken? Even your repentance bears witness to your origin. The false self may resist Him, but beneath every wandering desire lives the child who longs to come home. The Spirit does not persuade God to receive those who are not His own; He awakens God's children to the Father they have forgotten.

So do not fear to cry, "Abba, Father." Let that cry rise without hesitation. It is not the plea of an orphan hoping to be welcomed, but the voice of a child returning to the embrace for which they were created. The Father's purpose has never changed. He is restoring His likeness within you until you know, with unshakable joy, that you have always belonged in His house and always will.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Abba, Father, awaken Your life within me.*
