## 18 - The Wilderness Before the Way

> *The wilderness is not where the Father abandons His children. It is often where He prepares them to receive a deeper life.*

Our Lord did not enter the wilderness because He had wandered from the Father. He was led there by the Spirit. There are seasons when God seems silent, when familiar comforts disappear, and we feel the hunger of the soul more keenly than ever before. Such places are not signs of His absence. They are often the hidden workshops of His love, where deeper victories are won before they are ever seen.

Much of what passed between the Father and the Son in those forty days remains beyond our understanding. The deepest movements of the soul cannot always be spoken in plain words. Like seeds growing beneath the soil, God's greatest works are often hidden until the appointed hour. We should not be troubled when we cannot explain everything He is doing within us. The Father understands long before we do.

Only then does the hunger appear. Only then does the temptation become visible. It is often this way with us as well. After seasons of inward wrestling, the ordinary needs of life return, and it is there—in our weakness, our weariness, our longing—that the heart is tested. The question is never simply whether we are hungry, but whether we will seek life from something less than the Father. Every temptation is, at its root, an invitation to believe that life may be found somewhere other than in God.

Yet Christ has already walked this wilderness before us. He entered the lonely place not merely to show us how to endure it, but to fill even the wilderness with His own faithful presence. Because He trusted the Father where we have often failed, no desert need be a place of despair. Beneath every barren landscape runs the hidden river of God's life. Those who continue to trust Him will find that even the wilderness becomes holy ground, where hunger awakens a deeper appetite—not merely for bread, but for the living Word who gives Himself to us.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, nourish me with Your living presence.*
