## 34 - The One Who Is Good

> *The goodness we long for is not first a thing to achieve, but a Father to know. The closer we draw to Him, the more His life becomes our own.*

When the young ruler knelt before our Lord and called Him "Good Master," Jesus did not refuse goodness. Rather, He lifted the young man's eyes beyond Himself to the One from whom all goodness flows. The Son delighted to speak of His Father. His joy was never to gather praise for Himself, but to reveal the heart of the One who sent Him. If you would understand Christ, you must see that His every word, every deed, and every act of love opens a window into the Father's goodness.

Our Lord did not carry thoughts of Himself through the day. His heart rested continually in His Father. As a child instinctively turns toward home, so every movement of Christ's soul turned toward the Father's love. There He found His peace, His strength, and His delight. He came into the world not to establish His own glory, but to gather the Father's children back into the embrace they had forgotten. To see Jesus is to discover that God has always been better than we imagined.

The young man asked what good thing he must do to inherit eternal life. It is the question we often ask ourselves. We search for the right action, the right discipline, the right achievement that will finally satisfy the hunger within us. Yet eternal life is never a possession earned by doing. It is the life of God Himself shared with His children. It is not something you hold in your hands; it is a life that gently takes hold of your heart until all that you are begins to live from Him.

Do not spend your strength merely collecting good deeds, as though they alone could make you whole. Seek instead the living God, who alone is good. The branch does not struggle to produce fruit apart from the tree, nor does the child labor to manufacture the love of a father. Remain near the One whose goodness has no beginning and no end. As you behold Him, the desire to become like Him awakens, and what once felt like duty slowly becomes the joyful expression of a heart made alive by His own life.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, draw me into Your goodness until it becomes my own.*
