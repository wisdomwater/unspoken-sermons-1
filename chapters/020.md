## 20 - Living by the Father's Word

> *Life is not sustained first by what fills the body, but by the love that holds the soul. The Father's will is deeper nourishment than bread.*

Our Lord refused to turn the stone into bread, not because bread was beneath Him, nor because hunger was unholy. Hunger was real. Bread was good. Yet He would not grasp for life apart from the Father's hand. The Son would rather trust than seize, rather wait than possess. For to live by faith in the Father is a richer life than any miracle performed for self-preservation.

The temptation was not to satisfy hunger but to stop trusting. It suggested that the Father had forgotten His beloved Son, and that He must now provide for Himself. Every one of us hears this same whisper in different forms. *Take control. Secure yourself. Do not wait. Do not trust.* Yet every attempt to become our own provider slowly draws the heart away from the quiet freedom of being a beloved child.

Our true life has never depended first upon bread, success, health, or even pleasant feelings. These are gifts, and we receive them with gratitude when the Father gives them. But they are not the source of life. The deepest life comes from every word that proceeds from God—from His living presence speaking within the soul, assuring us that we belong to Him. Even when every outward comfort is removed, the person who abides in that Word has lost nothing essential.

The Father does not merely give us instructions; He gives us Himself. His living Word is not only written upon a page but spoken into the depths of our being by His Spirit. As we learn to trust that inward voice above every anxious craving, we discover a strange freedom. We no longer live by what we possess or by what we feel. We live because the Father loves us. And His love is nourishment that neither hunger nor death can ever exhaust.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to live by Your living Word.*
