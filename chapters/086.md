## 86 - The Justice of the Father

> *God's justice is not opposed to His mercy. His justice is mercy perfectly expressed, for it seeks not merely to punish what is wrong, but to make what is wrong right again.*

When we speak of the justice of God, we too quickly imagine a judge measuring out punishment. Yet punishment alone cannot restore what has been broken. If a thief is imprisoned, the wound he caused still remains. Justice has not reached its goal until what was torn apart begins to be made whole again. The Father's justice is therefore deeper than mere penalty. It seeks restoration. It labors until truth, love, and right relationship flourish where falsehood once reigned.

Think how your own heart responds when one who has wronged you truly repents. Their confession, their desire to make amends, their return to honesty awakens something greater than the satisfaction of seeing them suffer. The restored brother is a richer gift than the restored possession. This is no weakness of mercy. It is the very triumph of justice, for the person who committed the wrong has begun to become the person they were created to be.

So never imagine mercy and justice standing against one another within God, as though one part of Him wishes to forgive while another insists on punishment. The Father is not divided against Himself. His mercy is always just because it never ignores evil, and His justice is always merciful because it never delights in suffering. Whatever He does, He does for the healing of His children and the restoration of His creation. His judgments are not expressions of wounded pride but of perfect love refusing to abandon what it has made.

This is why we must be careful whenever someone tells us that God acts in ways no honorable person ever would. If our understanding of Scripture paints the Father as less just, less merciful, or less truthful than the goodness He has already awakened in our own conscience, then we have misunderstood either the passage or the Father. Jesus came to reveal God, and in Him we find no contradiction between righteousness and compassion. The One who welcomed sinners, confronted evil, and gave Himself for His enemies is the perfect image of the Father's justice.

Trust, then, that the justice of God is never against you, but always against the falsehood that keeps you from becoming fully alive. He will give every soul what perfect love knows is needed. He will never cease until wrong has been faced, truth has been embraced, and His children stand whole before Him. Such justice is not something to fear. It is one more reason to hope.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your just love make me whole.*
