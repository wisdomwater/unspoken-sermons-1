## 62 - The Father's Delight

> *Every true need within you is an invitation from the Father. The One who created your hunger is already preparing its fulfillment.*

Do not be ashamed of your need. It is no accident that your heart longs for life, truth, love, justice, and peace. The Father Himself planted these desires within you. He did not create you to be self-sufficient, but to live from Him. Every hunger of the soul bears witness that you belong to One who delights to satisfy it. Your need is not a flaw in His creation; it is part of His design, drawing you home to the heart from which you came.

Some imagine that God owes His creatures nothing, as though His greatness were measured by how little He is bound to love. But what father, worthy of the name, turns away from the cry of his child? The Father revealed in Jesus is infinitely nearer to us than any earthly parent could ever be. He is the source of our very being. If He has given us a thirst for goodness, it is because He Himself is the living water. If He has awakened in us a longing for home, it is because His own heart is our true home. His greatest desire is that His children should come boldly, asking Him for all He created them to receive.

The whole story of Christ is the Father's answer to our deepest necessity. Before we ever learned to pray, His love was already moving toward us. The Son came not to persuade an unwilling God to care, but to reveal the heart that has always cared without measure. In Jesus we behold what God has always been: absolute love, perfect righteousness, and joyful self-giving. There is no hidden severity behind the face of Christ. The Father's nature has never been less beautiful than the Son has shown it to be.

When sorrow or confusion makes God seem distant, hold fast to this: your existence itself is a claim upon His love. He fashioned you with purpose, and He will not abandon the work of His hands. Even suffering cannot dissolve the bond between Father and child. Indeed, He may use every trial to awaken within you a deeper longing for Himself, until at last you can say with Christ, "I come to do Your will." This is your birthright—not merely to receive gifts from God, but to share His very life, becoming ever more the son or daughter He created you to be.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, awaken every longing that leads me home to You.*
