## 37 - The Hardness of the Way

> *The way is hard only where we are unwilling to be made free. Christ does not ask for our possessions first, but for the heart that still believes it can live without Him.*

Many hear the Lord’s words to the rich young man and feel that He has been severe. Why should wealth make the kingdom difficult? Yet the difficulty does not arise from any prejudice in God against comfort or abundance. The kingdom of heaven receives the rich man as gladly as the poor, but it receives him as a man—not as a rich man. He cannot carry into the Father’s house the rank, security, and distinction his possessions gave him in the world. At the door of life, every child enters empty-handed.

We are often willing to give God something, provided He does not ask for the thing upon which we secretly rest. We would serve Him with our wealth, our talent, our influence, or our strength, yet hesitate to let Him become our wealth, our strength, and our life. We hope to keep the best of earth while gaining enough of heaven to feel secure. But heaven cannot be added to a life ordered around lesser treasures. The lower must become the servant of the higher, or it will quietly become our master.

Do not trouble yourself too soon with the question of whether you must sell all you possess. First ask whether you are doing the good already made plain to you. Are you truthful? Do you forgive? Do you honour those entrusted to your care? Do you love your neighbour? Do you seek justice and mercy? The Lord does not call us into imagined sacrifices while we neglect present obedience. He begins with the light already given, because obedience to that light prepares the eyes for more.

Yet obedience will eventually uncover a deeper hunger. You will discover that duty alone cannot make you whole. You may do much that is right and still feel within you the poverty of a heart not yet fully alive. Then you will begin to desire not merely salvation from consequence, but salvation from all that is false within you. You will no longer ask how little you may surrender and still be safe. You will ask what remains between you and perfect freedom.

When that hour comes, the Lord may touch the very thing you thought essential to your happiness. Count it no cruelty. It is a high honour to be shown the chain you are ready to break. Christ does not desire your money, but your freedom. He does not wish to impoverish you, but to give you Himself in place of everything that cannot last. Hard is the way only to the self that refuses to die. To the child longing for the Father, every surrender is a door opening into life.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, loosen my hold on all that keeps me from You.*
