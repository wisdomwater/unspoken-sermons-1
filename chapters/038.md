## 38 - The Hardness of the Way

> *Salvation is not the assurance that life will be ours someday, but the slow awakening of God’s own life within us now.*

A man may say that he does not trust in riches while depending upon them for peace, identity, and safety. He may speak instead of trusting in Christ’s merits, His sacrifice, or His finished work, yet still avoid trusting Christ Himself. But eternal life is not given by holding the correct account of what Jesus has done. Eternal life is to know the living God—to receive His being into ours until His will becomes our freedom and His love becomes our life. No doctrine about Christ can take the place of following the Man Himself.

“Children, how hard it is to enter the kingdom of God!” The Lord does not soften the saying. The difficulty belongs not only to the rich, but to every soul that must be born from above. We shrink from the death of the false self, from the clear air of truth, from the love that leaves no corner of the heart untouched. We desire comfort, certainty, and the promise of heaven, but the Father desires to make us heavenly. We ask to be assured that we are saved; He intends actually to save us.

Do not mistake the beginning of life for its fullness. Peace may come suddenly. Joy may overflow. Grace may enter the heart like dawn breaking over the hills. Yet salvation is nothing less than becoming one with God in goodness, truth, and love. Until we love as Christ loves, the life of heaven has not finished its work in us. Life may indeed have entered us, but we are still entering life.

For this reason the way continually opens beyond us. Just when we think we have reached the summit, the clouds part and reveal another shining height. This is not meant to discourage us. It is proof that we have climbed. We now see what once lay beyond our sight. The growing soul discovers ever more clearly how unlike Christ it remains, yet the same vision awakens hope: one day we shall be like Him, because we shall see Him as He is.

The things of this world are given to train us, not to possess us. They are signs, vessels, and shadows of realities greater than themselves. Beauty, shelter, food, strength, and wealth may all reveal something of the Father’s goodness, but we go astray when we cling to the vessel and forget the life it carries. You were not made to depend upon what can be shaken. You were made to possess the unseen things—love, truth, freedom, and fellowship with God. Hold every earthly gift with an open hand, and you will discover that even when all else is taken, the child of the Eternal still possesses everything.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, make Your life my only treasure.*
