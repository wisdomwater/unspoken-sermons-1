## 2 - The Heart of a Child

> *The heart that can recognize the beauty of a child is already awakening to the beauty of Christ. For the Son has not outgrown childlikeness, but reveals it as the very life of God.*

When our Lord placed a child in the midst of His disciples, He was not asking them merely to be kind to children. He was unveiling something hidden in the very nature of His kingdom. A child bears a likeness to Christ—not because a child is complete, but because there is a humble openness, a trusting spirit, and an unguarded heart that reflects something eternal. Whoever welcomes such a one has begun to recognize the very character of the Son.

This is why Jesus says that whoever receives Him receives the Father who sent Him. The Son perfectly reveals the Father because there is no distance between Their hearts. To know Christ is to know the Father's goodness. And if Christ Himself delights to be known through the image of a child, then we should not imagine that God is less gentle than His Son. The tenderness we see in Jesus is not a passing mood but the everlasting nature of the Father.

We spend much of our lives trying to become important. We measure ourselves against one another, hoping to stand a little higher or be noticed a little more. Yet the kingdom of heaven is built upon an entirely different foundation. Its King stoops to serve. Its greatness is found in humility. Its honor is to give rather than to grasp. In such a kingdom, the one most like the King is not the one who rises above others, but the one who loves without thought of rank.

As you learn to cherish the childlike spirit wherever you encounter it—in a little one, in a weary soul made simple by suffering, or in the quiet longing within your own heart—you are learning to recognize Christ Himself. And as you receive Christ, you discover that you have been welcomed into the embrace of the Father. This is the kingdom's secret: the King does not keep His subjects at a distance. He gathers them into His arms, restoring them to the joyful simplicity for which they were created.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, make my heart like Yours—humble, trusting, and full of love.*
