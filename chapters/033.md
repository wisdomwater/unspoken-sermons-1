## 33 - The Resurrection of Love

> *The Father does not redeem souls only. He redeems persons, that love may behold love again and rejoice forever.*

God made us for revelation. Through these bodies we have learned wonder, sorrow, beauty, friendship, and love. Through eyes we have gazed upon creation. Through hands we have comforted and been comforted. Through voices we have spoken truth and heard words that awakened our hearts. None of these gifts are accidents. They are the Father's good design, teaching us His life through the world He has made.

For this reason, our hope is not that we should escape creation, but that creation itself should be fulfilled. The resurrection is not the recovery of worn-out flesh, but the Father's promise that nothing true will be lost. As a seed becomes more fully itself when it flowers, so shall we become more ourselves in the life to come. Every shadow will fall away. Every falsehood will perish. What is most truly *you* will shine with a beauty that has long been hidden beneath weakness and mortality.

Nor is this hope only for ourselves. Love longs to know and to be known. The Father who teaches us to love one another will not ask us to forget those whom He Himself has joined to our hearts. The faces we have cherished, the voices that have shaped us, the fellowship that has drawn us nearer to God—all these belong to His good purpose. When His work is complete, we shall not love one another less, but more. We shall recognize one another not by fading memory, but by the fuller glory of who each person was always created to become.

What joy it will be to discover that every soul is more itself than ever before. The timid will be fearless, the broken made whole, the weary radiant with life. We shall look upon those we have loved and say, "There you are—at last, entirely yourself." In seeing one another rightly, we shall see more deeply the beauty of Christ reflected in every restored child of the Father.

This is the resurrection toward which all creation waits. God is not gathering His children into an endless solitude, but into a family where every person reveals His goodness more clearly than before. In that kingdom, nothing that love has made holy will be lost. The Father will unveil His children to one another, and together we shall behold His face with hearts made perfectly alive.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, bring all Your children into the fullness of life and love.*
