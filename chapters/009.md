## 9 - The Faith of Hopeful Children

> *The truest faith is not the certainty that we have understood everything, but the confidence that the Father's goodness is greater than all we have yet imagined.*

Blessed are those who can believe before every mystery has been explained. They do not mistake wonder for fantasy or mystery for confusion. Instead, they ask a simpler and wiser question: *Is this like the Father I have come to know in Christ?* Where they discern His goodness, they are willing to wait patiently for fuller understanding. Their confidence rests not in the completeness of their knowledge, but in the faithfulness of the One they seek.

Such faith does not shrink God's mercy to the measure of human expectation. We may stretch our hopes as far as imagination can carry them, yet we shall never outrun the generosity of God. His thoughts rise above ours as the heavens above the earth. His love reaches farther than our prayers know how to ask. We need never fear that our highest hope will exceed His highest purpose, for His heart has always conceived blessings beyond the dreams of His children.

Therefore bring your whole life before Him. Hide nothing. Carry your joys, your griefs, your questions, your failures, your longings—all of them—into His presence. There is no corner of your soul where He is unwilling to dwell, no honest question He despises, no burden He refuses to bear. If the answer must wait, it is not because love has forgotten you, but because love is patiently preparing you to receive more than you can yet understand.

Walk, then, as a hopeful child. Confess freely what you do not know, but never doubt the goodness of the One who does know. One day you will look back with wonder, not that you trusted God too much, but that you trusted Him so little. For when His purposes are finally revealed, every faithful hope will discover that it rested upon a love greater than it ever dared imagine.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let my hope rest in the greatness of Your love.*
