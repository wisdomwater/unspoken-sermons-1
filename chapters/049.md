## 49 - The Last Farthing

> *God's justice is not the determination to punish, but the determination to make His children wholly true. Love will never rest until every falsehood has given way to righteousness.*

Our Lord's words are not puzzles meant to satisfy curiosity but living seeds meant to awaken the heart. They were never given merely to be explained, but to be obeyed. A willing soul will often understand more through a single act of faithfulness than another gains through years of argument. The light of God is given to those who walk by it. Truth grows brighter as it is lived.

When Jesus speaks of paying the last farthing, He is not delighting in threats but revealing the unwavering faithfulness of divine love. The Father does not overlook what is false, nor does He grow weary of what is right. Whatever remains unlike Him must one day be surrendered. This is not because He longs to exact payment, but because He longs to restore His children completely. Perfect love can never be satisfied with half-healed hearts.

How wonderful, then, that God's justice is inseparable from His goodness. If He could be persuaded to ignore evil, there would be no hope for us. The promise is not that righteousness may somehow be avoided, but that it will at last become our own joy. The Father is determined that every son and daughter shall one day love what He loves and freely choose the good. Whatever this healing costs, He will not abandon the work before it is complete.

Therefore do not seek escape from righteousness, but surrender to it. Christ has not come merely to cover what is false within us but to make us true. His life becomes our righteousness as His Spirit awakens our own willing participation in the life of God. Every victory over selfishness, every honest confession, every step toward love is already the Father's kingdom growing within you. The last farthing will indeed be paid—not because love has failed, but because love refuses to leave even the smallest shadow unredeemed.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, make me true as You are true.*
