## 70 - Faith That Follows

> *Faith is not chiefly believing something about Christ. Faith is trusting Christ enough to do what He says.*

How easily we mistake the living Lord for our ideas about Him. We imagine that if our thoughts are correct, our hearts must also be right. Yet Christ never invited anyone merely to agree with Him. He called them to follow Him. He did not say, "Think rightly about Me," but, "Come after Me." The difference is immense. One may admire the map while never taking the journey. The other walks beside the Guide until the destination becomes home.

Do not rest your hope upon any theory, however carefully formed. Rest it upon the living Christ. His work cannot be separated from His person, nor His gifts from His presence. The One who healed the sick still heals the soul—not as a distant memory to be analyzed, but as the risen Lord who walks among His people today. Salvation is not confidence in an explanation. It is confidence in the Savior Himself.

If you wonder whether you truly believe, spend less time examining your feelings and more time listening for His voice. Ask not first, "Do I possess enough faith?" but, "What has my Lord asked of me today?" Then obey the next clear word you already know. Forgive the one who has wronged you. Speak truth with kindness. Give generously. Pray for your enemy. Receive the forgotten. The smallest act of obedience opens the heart more deeply to Christ than endless anxious questioning about whether one believes correctly.

This is the beginning and the growth of faith. Every step of obedience leads to a clearer vision of the One who calls us. As we walk with Him, our misunderstandings gradually fade, for the Lord Himself becomes our teacher. He is patient with those who seek Him honestly. Therefore, leave behind the endless labor of trying to prove your faith to yourself. Follow the Son. Trust Him enough to do what He says. In that living fellowship, truth ceases to be merely believed—it becomes the very life of your soul.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Lord Jesus, teach me to trust You by following You.*
