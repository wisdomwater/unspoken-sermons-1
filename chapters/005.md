## 5 - From Fear to Love

> *God meets us where we are, but He loves us too much to leave us there. Even fear may become the doorway through which love leads us home.*

When God revealed Himself in fire upon the mountain, the people could see little beyond His terrible majesty. They were a nation only recently freed from slavery, their hearts still shaped by fear and their desires still reaching back toward Egypt. Such souls could scarcely imagine that the fire before them was anything other than judgment. Yet God did not deceive them. The fire truly belonged to Him. It revealed His holiness, though they could only perceive a small part of its meaning.

So it is with every revelation of God. We never see the whole at once. The Father speaks according to what His children are able to receive, not because His heart changes, but because ours is still learning to see. What begins as trembling before His greatness is meant to grow into joyful confidence in His goodness. The truth unfolds as we are transformed by it. Every glimpse awakens a deeper longing to know Him more.

There is a time when fear is better than indifference. It is better to know that evil cannot dwell with God than to imagine that evil is of no consequence. Yet fear is never the destination. The Father does not desire children who forever stand at the foot of the mountain, afraid to draw near. He desires sons and daughters who discover that the fire they feared is the very fire that heals them. The holiness that once seemed against them is, in truth, against everything that keeps them from becoming fully alive.

As long as we cling to our selfishness, God's love will feel like resistance. We mistake our sins for ourselves and imagine that whatever opposes them must oppose us. But the Father's heart has never been against His children. He wars only against the darkness that binds them. His consuming fire divides the evil from the beloved, destroys the false without harming the true, and will not rest until all that remains is the life He always intended to share with us. One day we shall no longer flee His fire, but rejoice to stand within its everlasting light.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, lead me from fear into the freedom of Your love.*
