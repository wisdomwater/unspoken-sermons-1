## 67 - The Yoke of the Son

> *To follow Christ is not merely to admire Him but to share His life. His path is the Father's will, and His joy is to lead us into that same freedom.*

When the Lord says, "Follow Me," He is not asking us to adopt a system of thought or to defend a doctrine. He is walking toward the Father, and He invites us to walk beside Him. His whole life is one continual "Yes" to the Father's will. To believe in Him, then, is not first to think correctly about Him, but to enter into that same obedience of love. We learn Christ not by studying Him from afar, but by keeping company with Him until His mind becomes our own.

Be watchful, however, of the self, for it is subtle beyond measure. Even when it appears to have surrendered, it may secretly delight in its own humility, its own sacrifices, its own holiness. It gladly exchanges one crown for another if only it may still reign. Therefore, do not trust even your victories over yourself. Trust only the God who alone can cleanse the heart. Self cannot cure self. Only the indwelling Christ can quietly empty the throne until the Father alone is King within.

This is why the Lord calls us to take up our cross every day. The cross is not merely suffering that comes upon us, nor every inconvenience we happen to endure. It is the daily choosing of the Father's will over the restless claims of the false self. Again and again we lay down our demand to be first, to be praised, to be secure, to have our own way. And again and again the life of Christ rises within us, making what once felt impossible become natural. The yoke grows light because we discover that we are carrying the very yoke He Himself carries—the joyful burden of the Father's will.

Do not be afraid of losing yourself in such a life. The self that clings to itself grows smaller with every grasp. The self surrendered into the hands of God is enlarged beyond imagining. What seems like loss becomes true discovery. You cease to be your own anxious guardian and become the beloved child of the Father, resting in His care. There is no greater freedom than to know that God's will and your deepest joy are no longer two different things, but one. This is the life Christ came to share with us—the life of sons and daughters who delight to dwell in the Father's heart.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your will become the joy of my heart.*
