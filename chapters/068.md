## 68 - The Joy Beyond Self

> *The self that clings to itself grows smaller. The self surrendered to God is returned radiant, enlarged, and filled with the joy of divine love.*

The Lord never asks us to surrender anything without intending to restore it in a truer and more beautiful form. When we deny the false self, we do not cease to be ourselves. Rather, the Father takes the poor and broken consciousness we have fashioned through fear and pride, carries it into the workshop of His love, and returns it cleansed, radiant, and fit to share His own life forever. Nothing real is lost. Only what could never satisfy is left behind.

This is why the joys of God's kingdom are not the rewards of selfishness but the flowering of love. Every good gift—beauty, friendship, learning, work, creation itself—is received anew when it is received from the Father's hand instead of grasped as though it came by chance. The child sees every blessing as the smile of the Giver. Gratitude transforms possession into communion. What once ended in the self now becomes another doorway into the heart of God.

Nor should you fear that holiness leaves no room for delight. Joy is not the enemy of self-denial but its fulfillment. The Father created His children for gladness. Yet only the heart that has ceased making itself the center can bear the weight of such happiness. Heaven is not the triumph of private bliss but the endless exchange of love, where each delights in the good of all, and all rejoice together in God. The more love forgets itself, the more joy overflows.

Therefore, hold every earthly gift with open hands. Love your family deeply, cherish beauty, welcome knowledge, enjoy the goodness of creation—but never let any good thing stand between your heart and the Father. If He asks you to lay something aside, it is never because He despises His gifts, but because He intends to return them purified, enlarged, and woven into His eternal kingdom. Follow Christ above every opinion, every comfort, every cherished certainty. For the Truth is not an idea to defend but a living Lord to follow. And whoever loses everything for His sake will one day discover that nothing worth having has been lost at all.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to receive every good thing from Your hand alone.*
