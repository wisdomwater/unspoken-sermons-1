## 63 - The Rights of a Child

> *The Father owes you nothing that love has not already promised. Every gift He gives, every wound He permits, and every mercy He withholds is governed by one unchanging purpose—to make you fully alive in Him.*

Do not mistake your rights as a child of God for merits you have earned. You have earned nothing, and yet you have been given everything. Your claim upon the Father does not rise from your goodness but from His. He created you for Himself, and having done so, He will not cease His work until you become all that His love intended. What He began in love, He will complete in love.

Therefore, do not fear even the Father's discipline. If pain unmasks your pride, if disappointment loosens your grip upon lesser things, if every false refuge collapses until you find no resting place but God Himself, then even these sorrows are among your birthrights. Better the sharp kindness that leads you home than the gentle lie that leaves you wandering. The Good Shepherd loves His sheep too well to let them be content among empty husks. He will pursue them through every dark valley until they discover that nothing can satisfy the heart but His own life dwelling within it.

When you look upon Jesus Christ, all uncertainty about the Father's heart must come to an end. There is no hidden God behind Him, more reluctant to forgive or less eager to bless. Christ is the Father's own heart made visible. He did not persuade God to become loving; He revealed the love that has always been. The cross is not the beginning of divine compassion but its fullest unveiling. In Christ we see that God's justice and God's mercy are not rivals. His justice is the unwavering determination to destroy all that destroys His children, while His mercy never ceases until those children themselves are restored.

And so, when you cannot understand the Father's ways, do not demand explanations before you trust Him. Job was not given the reason for his suffering; he was given a greater vision of the One who held him. Often the deepest answer God offers is not an argument but Himself. The One who painted the heavens, shaped every flower, and knows the paths of every living creature is also the Father who knows your heart better than you know it yourself. To behold Him is the beginning of peace. As you learn to see His face in Christ, you will discover that the whole creation has been quietly proclaiming the same truth all along: Love is deeper than your questions, and the Father is better than you have yet imagined.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, lead me through every trial into the fullness of Your life.*
