## 46 - The Freedom of the Father

> *The Father's love is never imprisoned by His own creation. His wisdom has always made room for the prayers of His children.*

Some imagine that God's wisdom leaves no place for prayer, as though everything had been settled before we ever drew breath. But what kind of Father would prepare a world for His children and leave no room for their voices? The One who calls us into freedom has not bound Himself with lifeless machinery. His purposes never change, for they are always love, yet within that love He delights to welcome the living fellowship of His sons and daughters. Prayer is not an interruption to His work; it is one of the very reasons His work exists.

Look at the world He has made. No two sunsets are alike, no season repeats itself exactly, no life unfolds by rigid pattern alone. Everywhere His creation speaks of order filled with freedom, harmony alive with fresh beauty. So it is with the Father Himself. His faithfulness is unchanging, yet His love is living, personal, and responsive. He is no distant force pushing the universe onward, but the ever-present Life within it, attending each of His children with perfect care.

Our Lord revealed this beautiful freedom at the wedding in Cana. His mother's request was not part of what He had first intended for that hour, yet in love He made room for her desire without departing from His Father's will. Here we glimpse the heart of God. The Father is not reluctant to hear His children, nor too occupied with greater matters to notice their smaller joys. Divine love is never lessened by tenderness. Indeed, its greatness is shown in its willingness to stoop close enough to listen.

Therefore do not imagine that your prayers disappear into an unyielding universe. The Father has always known they would come. He has woven into His wise governance room for the growing hearts of His children, that they might learn not merely to receive His gifts but to share His life. Every prayer offered in trust becomes another thread binding the child more closely to the Father's heart, where freedom and love are forever one.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to trust Your living love.*
