## 11 - Christ Is God's Forgiveness

> *The Father does not forgive by overlooking evil, but by overcoming it with love. His forgiveness is not the denial of our wound, but its healing.*

When we think of forgiveness, we often imagine that God simply chooses not to remember our sin. But the Father's forgiveness is far more beautiful than that. He does not pretend evil never happened, nor does He leave us imprisoned beneath its weight. His love moves toward us with one purpose: to remove whatever stands between His heart and ours. This is why Christ is God's forgiveness made visible. In Him, the Father comes to destroy all that separates us from His life.

A loving father does not treat every wound in the same way. A careless word may be met with a tender embrace that melts the child's sorrow at once. But a deeper sickness—a heart growing proud, cruel, or deceitful—requires a stronger love. Such love cannot ignore the evil because it seeks the child, not merely the child's comfort. What appears as discipline is often love refusing to abandon the beloved to what would destroy them.

So it is with our heavenly Father. His forgiveness is never indifference. It is love pursuing us until the false self begins to yield. Long before we turn toward Him, His love is already at work, clearing away the fog our sin has raised, sending sunlight, daily mercies, and quiet invitations that awaken repentance. He forgives us before we know how to receive forgiveness, patiently preparing our hearts until they can open again like the hearts of little children.

The day comes when His love reaches the place we have guarded so fiercely. Pride dissolves into tears. Shame gives way to trust. We discover that forgiveness was never merely a sentence spoken over us, but the Father's own life entering us, driving out the darkness that could not remain in His presence. As His restoring work is completed, nothing remains between us. God dwells in us, and we in Him. This has always been the destination of His forgiveness.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your forgiving love make me whole.*
