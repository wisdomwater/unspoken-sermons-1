## 23 - Love Goes to the Root

> *God does not overcome evil by greater force, but by deeper love. He does not merely restrain what is wrong; He restores what has been lost.*

How easily our Lord could have become the king the people desired. He could have gathered armies, overthrown oppressors, and established justice with irresistible power. To human eyes, it would have seemed the triumph of righteousness. Yet He saw farther than appearances. The Father's purpose was never simply to defeat evil from the outside, but to heal the human heart from within.

For what good is it to overthrow one tyrant if the love of power still reigns in the soul? What freedom is won if resentment merely changes hands? The Father desires something greater than new rulers or better circumstances. He longs to restore His own image in every person—the oppressed and the oppressor alike. His love reaches even toward those whose hearts have become hardened, for He will not rest until every child has been invited home.

This is why Christ chose the cross instead of the throne. He laid the axe not to the branches of evil but to its root. Love entered the darkest places where fear, pride, hatred, and death believed themselves strongest, and there it began their undoing. The victory of God is patient because it is complete. He is willing to endure long ages of misunderstanding if, in the end, His children become truly free—not merely free to do as they please, but free to become all He created them to be.

There are moments when we wish God would simply fix the world at once. Yet the Father has never abandoned His purpose. His work continues quietly, steadily, irresistibly. Every act of forgiveness weakens the kingdom of darkness. Every surrender to love strikes another blow at the root of evil. And because Christ remained faithful even when He felt forsaken, we may trust that no darkness, no delay, and no sorrow can finally overcome the goodness of God. His love is stronger than death, and therefore His restoration will have the final word.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, root out all that is not love, and restore Your life within me.*
