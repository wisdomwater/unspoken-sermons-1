## 94 - The Tears of Life

> *Jesus wept not because Lazarus had vanished into death, but because those He loved could not yet see beyond it. His tears were the sorrow of Life standing among those who believed the grave had won.*

When Jesus came to the tomb of Lazarus, He found Martha and Mary overcome with grief. Their sorrow was real, and He did not despise it. Yet beneath their tears lay something that troubled Him deeply. They mourned as though Lazarus had fallen out of the Father’s hands, as though death had carried him into a darkness where love could no longer reach. The One who had come to reveal the God of the living stood beside them, but they could see only the grave.

The indignation of Jesus was not anger against their love for Lazarus. God does not ask us to love one another less so that we may love Him more. He gives us to one another for eternal belonging, and every true affection has its source in Him. Jesus was troubled because their grief had become a veil hiding the Father. They had Lazarus no longer before their eyes, and so it seemed to them that they no longer had Lazarus at all.

Yet Jesus could see what they could not. On one side stood the sisters weeping beside a sealed tomb; on the other was Lazarus alive in the keeping of God. The Lord stood between the visible and the unseen, knowing both to be held within one life. Death appeared to Martha and Mary as a wall, but to Jesus it was only a door. Their brother had not ceased to be. He had gone where their sight could not follow, but not where the Father’s love could not hold him.

Then Jesus wept. His tears were not the surrender of hope but the overflowing of divine compassion. He was grieved by their grief and wounded by the unbelief that made their sorrow unbearable. He did not rebuke them. He turned toward the Father, gave thanks, and called Lazarus back—not because Lazarus had been lost, but because those who remained needed to know that he had never been beyond the reach of Life.

So the man came forth, still wrapped in the garments of the grave, a living testimony that death is not lord. Jesus commanded that he be unbound, as though saying to every frightened heart: Do not bind the living with your belief in death. Those who have passed from your sight have not passed from God. The Father keeps what He loves, and nothing entrusted to Him can be lost.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to trust the Life beyond my sight.*
