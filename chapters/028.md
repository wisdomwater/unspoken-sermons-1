## 28 - Meeting One Another in God

> *We cannot love another by force of will alone. We learn to love as the life of God becomes our own.*

Many of us have sincerely tried to love our neighbor only to discover how quickly our love reaches its limits. It is easy enough to love those whose kindness naturally awakens affection within us. But what of the ordinary, the difficult, the distant, or the one who stirs nothing in us at all? We soon discover that love cannot be manufactured. The command reveals our need for something greater than determination.

The Father never intended us to produce His love from our own small resources. Love is His life before it is our achievement. As a branch cannot bear fruit apart from the vine, so we cannot truly love apart from the life of Christ flowing within us. The nearer we draw to God, the nearer we find ourselves drawn to one another. In Him, hearts that seem separated discover that they belong to the same family.

This is why our Lord could command us to love without exaggeration. He was not asking the impossible. He was revealing the destiny toward which the whole creation is moving. Every soul is being invited into the life of the Son, where the Father's love becomes our own. What seems beyond us today becomes possible as His life quietly transforms ours.

When you find your heart unable to love, do not condemn yourself, nor merely strive harder. Turn again to the Father. Ask Him to fill you with the mind of Christ. The love you seek for your neighbor is found first in the One who already loves both of you perfectly. There, in His presence, the walls of isolation begin to fall, and we discover that true individuality does not separate us—it gives us the freedom to love one another as beloved children of the same Father.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your love become the life within me.*
