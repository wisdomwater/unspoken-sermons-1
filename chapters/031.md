## 31 - The Brother Hidden Within

> *Love does not call evil good. It sees through evil to the child of God waiting to be restored, and refuses to forget who that person truly is.*

Our Lord does not ask the impossible when He tells us to love our enemies. He invites us into the very life of the Father. The Father causes His sun to rise on the evil and the good alike because His love is not governed by the worthiness of its object, but by the goodness of His own heart. To become His child is to find that same love awakening within us—not by striving harder, but by sharing in His life.

We often think we are commanded to love what is hateful. But cruelty is not lovable, nor is deceit, pride, or contempt. These are not the true person. They are the sickness that has grown over the soul, like ivy hiding the beauty of an old stone wall. Christ never asks us to embrace the darkness. He asks us to remember the light that still belongs to the one who has wandered far from it.

Every enemy is, before all else, a son or daughter made for the Father's house. Beneath the scars of sin and the distortions of selfishness remains a humanity that God has never ceased to love. It may seem buried beneath years of hardness, but it is not lost to Him. The Father looks beyond what sin has made of a person and sees what His love intends to restore. If we would see with His eyes, we too must learn to look deeper than the injury we have received.

This is why hatred can never heal the world. Hatred fixes a person forever in the shape of their worst moment. Love refuses such a verdict. Love waits. Love hopes. Love surrounds the wounded soul, believing that one day the imprisoned brother or forgotten sister may awaken and answer love with love. This hope is not naive; it is born from confidence in the relentless goodness of the Father, whose purpose is always to restore His children into His own likeness.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to see every person through Your restoring love.*
