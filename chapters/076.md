## 76 - The Life That Must Become Ours

> *Christ did not come only to shine before us as the Light. He came to awaken His own life within us, until we too can say, "Father, Your will be done."*

The light of Christ is not the destination; it is the invitation. We behold His life so that His life may become our own. The Son did not merely teach surrender—He lived it. His whole existence was one continual offering to the Father. Now He calls us to walk the same path. Eternal life begins wherever a heart freely says, "Father, I am Yours."

No one can surrender your will for you. Christ has gone before you, His Spirit strengthens you, and His grace surrounds you, but the sacred "yes" must still arise from within your own heart. God never forces His children into sonship. Love cannot be compelled. The Father patiently waits until His child willingly entrusts everything into His hands. In that surrender we lose nothing that is truly ours. We discover who we have always been.

There will be seasons when God seems hidden, when prayers feel unanswered and the soul stands in the cold rain outside the house. Do not mistake those moments for abandonment. The peace between the Father and the Son has never been broken, and because Christ holds us within that eternal love, all shall ultimately be well. If the winter lingers, it is only because deeper roots are growing. The Gardener has not forgotten His garden.

So do not lose heart when the way is difficult. Keep turning your face toward the Father. Trust the Son who walked this road before you. The day will come when the surrender that now feels costly will become pure delight, and the life of Christ will blossom within you as your own true life. Then His prayer will be fulfilled in you, and you will know by joyful experience that the Father's will was never your burden—it was always your freedom.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your will become the deepest joy of my heart.*
