## 32 - The God of the Living

> *God does not remember His children as one recalls what is gone. He knows them as One who never ceases to give them life.*

When our Lord declared that God is "not the God of the dead, but of the living," He was revealing far more than a truth about life after death. He was unveiling the very heart of the Father. The One who calls Himself the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob does not speak of them as memories preserved in heaven. He speaks of them as those who still belong to Him, for His love cannot lose what it has once truly claimed.

The Father's life is not interrupted by what we call death. Those who disappear from our sight never disappear from His. The eyes that first imagined you into being have never ceased to behold you. If His thought called you into existence, shall His love suddenly forget you? Impossible. Every soul lives because it is continually held within the faithful gaze of the Living God.

This hope rests not upon our strength, but upon His character. A God who creates only to abandon His children would not be the Father revealed in Jesus Christ. The One who delights to give life will not cease His work halfway. What He begins, He intends to complete. His covenant is not measured by the few years we spend on earth, but by His own eternal faithfulness, which knows no ending.

Even the resurrection is a further expression of this generous love. God does not merely preserve us as spirits hidden away from creation. He desires that we should once more know one another, delight in His world, and behold His glory with renewed fullness. The Father's purpose is never less than complete restoration. Life with Him is always moving toward greater revelation, deeper communion, and fuller joy.

Therefore, do not measure life by what your eyes can see. Those whom you love are not lost to the Father. Nor are you. Even now, your life is hidden with Christ in God. The Living One holds you every moment, and because He lives, nothing that belongs to His love can finally perish.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Living Father, keep me always in Your life.*
