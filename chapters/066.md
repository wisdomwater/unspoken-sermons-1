## 66 - Losing the False Self

> *The self is not destroyed by love but surrendered to it. We lose only the life that keeps us from the Life for which we were created.*

Christ does not call us to despise ourselves. He calls us to follow Him. There is a great difference. Many imagine that self-denial means making life as difficult as possible, refusing every pleasure, or measuring holiness by how much one suffers. But this is only another way for the self to remain at the center, secretly admiring its own sacrifice. The Father seeks something far deeper. He does not ask you to torment yourself. He asks you to entrust yourself to Him.

The self was never meant to be your master. It is a good servant but a miserable king. Left to itself, it asks only, *What do I want? What will make me secure? What will others think of me?* Yet the Son lived by another question altogether: *What is the Father's will?* His joy was not found in preserving Himself but in receiving everything from the Father's hand. This is the freedom into which He now invites us—not the slavery of constant self-improvement, but the peace of no longer making ourselves the center of our own lives.

As the life of God grows within us, we begin to discover that self-denial is not the loss of our true identity but the surrender of the false one. Pride loosens its grip. Ambition grows quiet. The hunger for praise fades. Fear of failure no longer rules us. We become less occupied with protecting ourselves and more free to love. Little by little, the Father's own life becomes the life of His children. What once felt like surrender is revealed to have been liberation all along.

Therefore, do not be anxious to preserve yourself. Receive every good gift with gratitude, release every gift when love asks it of you, and trust the Father in both abundance and loss. The more your heart learns to say, "Not my will, but Yours," the more you will discover that His will is not the enemy of your joy but its source. Christ loses nothing worth keeping, and neither will those who follow Him. In yielding the false self, they receive the true son or daughter they were always meant to become.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, free me from myself and fill me with Your life.*
