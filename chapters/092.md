## 92 - Come Into the Light

> *God does not condemn us because we have wandered into darkness, but because we refuse the hand that leads us home. His judgment is the steadfast love that will not abandon us to what destroys us.*

The light of God does not come into the world to expose us for shame but to call us into life. Every one of us has known darkness. We have chosen what wounded ourselves and others, often seeing too little to know the depth of the harm we caused. Yet this is not the heart of our condemnation. The Father does not stand over us keeping a ledger of every failure. His grief is that we cling to the darkness when the door into His light stands open before us.

Our Lord does not say that forgiveness is scarce. Rather, He declares it so abundant that no sin need keep us from returning. The only barrier is our unwillingness to leave behind the thing that is killing us. Love cannot bless what destroys its beloved. The Father cannot call evil good, for that would be to abandon His child to ruin. His refusal is not the hardness of anger but the firmness of perfect love.

Do not imagine that God delights in punishment. He desires healing, not suffering. Yet when gentleness is refused, love must become a fire that burns more deeply. Whatever pain comes from His hand has one purpose alone: to awaken the sleeping heart, loosen the chains of falsehood, and lead His child back into the freedom of truth. His judgments are never revenge. They are the persistent work of restoration, continuing for as long as love requires.

Nor should we presume to know how God has dealt with every soul. The Light has shone upon every person in some measure, yet each has received according to the wisdom of the Father. We are not judges of another's opportunity or response. Our concern is only this: when the light reaches us today, will we welcome it? Every excuse to remain as we are delays the joy that God longs to give.

So when the Father calls, do not fear His light. Bring Him the habit you cannot break, the wound you cannot heal, the sin you are ashamed to name. He already knows it all. What He seeks is not your perfection before you come, but your willingness to let Him lead you out. The One who is light will never cease His labor until His children stand in the full freedom for which they were created.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, lead me gladly out of darkness into Your light.*
