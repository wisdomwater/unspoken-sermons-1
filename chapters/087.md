## 87 - The Destruction of Sin

> *God does not punish because suffering balances sin. He punishes only because love will not leave His child in bondage to what destroys him.*

Punishment is not payment. No amount of suffering can undo a cruel word, restore a betrayed trust, or make an unjust act righteous. Pain placed in one scale cannot balance sin in the other, for they are not opposites. The opposite of sin is goodness. The answer to falsehood is truth. The path from wrong to right is not suffering by itself, but repentance.

Why, then, does the Father allow His children to suffer for their sins? Because He is determined to destroy the sin that is destroying them. His aim is not to satisfy resentment, nor to exact from the sinner an amount of pain equal to the wrong committed. Such a thing would mend nothing. God punishes only as a physician wounds to heal, as fire burns to purify, as light pains eyes long accustomed to darkness. Every stroke of His judgment is directed not against the person He loves, but against the evil that has made that person less than true.

The deepest punishment is not the fire outside a person, but the awakening within. When the one who has done wrong at last sees the ugliness of the deed, when he can no longer defend it, when he cries, “I hate what I have become; Father, make me clean,” then judgment has begun to accomplish its holy purpose. The sinner has taken God’s side against his own sin. Repentance is the first breaking of dawn, for the heart that loathes its falsehood has already begun to love the truth.

This is atonement: not pain endured instead of transformation, but enemies reconciled, wrong confessed, restitution begun, and love restored. Christ came to create this atonement in us. He did not die merely that we might escape the consequences of evil while remaining unchanged. He died and rose to bring us into unity with the Father and with one another. His cross is the revelation of a love strong enough to enter our darkness and remain there until we are willing to come home.

Trust, then, that God will spare no healing means necessary to deliver His child. He will not abandon the sinner to sin, nor preserve evil forever merely that punishment might continue. His justice must overcome what is false, and His mercy must restore what has been wounded. The Father’s victory is not that the wrongdoer suffers endlessly, but that the wrongdoer becomes righteous, makes what amends love permits, and rejoices at last in the goodness once resisted. Only then is sin truly defeated.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, destroy the sin and restore Your child.*
