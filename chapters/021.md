## 21 - The Quiet Strength of Trust

> *Faith does not ask God to prove His love. It rests because His love has already been revealed. The child of God seeks not signs, but the Father's will.*

Our Lord would not turn stones into bread to satisfy Himself, nor would He throw Himself from the temple to force His Father to act. In both temptations, His answer sprang from the same living root: the Father's will was His life. He did not need to compel God to prove His care, for He already knew the heart of the One who had sent Him. Such confidence is deeper than certainty of outcomes. It is the quiet assurance that the Father is always good, whether angels appear or silence remains.

Many of us long for unmistakable signs. We ask God to remove every uncertainty before we dare to walk forward. Yet love does not grow by demanding proof. A child who knows a father's heart does not continually test whether he will keep his promises. The relationship itself becomes the place of rest. So it is with our Father. He calls us not to examine His faithfulness but to live within it.

There will be seasons when His nearness seems hidden and His voice grows quiet. In those hours, faith is not sustained by emotion but by the truth we have already seen. Once the goodness of God has been revealed to the heart, we may continue to walk even when we no longer feel what we once felt. The light remembered becomes enough to guide us through the night, because God Himself has not changed.

The greatest question is never, "What will God do if I choose this path?" but, "Father, what would You have me do?" That question frees us from striving to control the future. We need not force God's hand or hurry His work. We are simply invited to follow where His Spirit leads, trusting that every step taken in obedience is held within His perfect love. Such trust is stronger than spectacle, steadier than certainty, and richer than any miracle we could demand.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to seek only Your will and to rest in Your unfailing goodness.*
