## 53 - Becoming Sons and Daughters

> *God does not merely call you His child; He is patiently forming you into the likeness of His own heart. The journey of faith is not toward acceptance, but toward communion.*

From the beginning, the Father has desired more than children who simply bear His name. He longs for sons and daughters who share His heart. We are His by creation, but His purpose reaches still deeper. He would have us grow into such fellowship with Him that we love what He loves, desire what He desires, and delight in His goodness as naturally as a child delights in the presence of a beloved father. This is the true maturity of the soul.

The work of Christ is not to persuade God to receive us but to awaken us into the life for which we were created. The Spirit teaches us to cry, "Abba, Father," not as strangers hoping for welcome, but as children learning at last to know the One who has always been their Father. What Scripture calls sonship is the coming of age of the heart. It is the slow and joyful discovery that obedience is not servitude but shared life. We become free when the Father's will becomes our own delight.

The Father never ceases to be faithful, even when we do not yet resemble Him. His love waits with infinite patience, making room for us to become. He does not force holiness upon us, for love cannot be compelled. Instead, He draws us into the truth, inviting us again and again to choose what is good until His life begins to blossom within our own. Little by little, the image that was marred is restored, and we find ourselves becoming at home in His presence.

Perhaps the word *Father* has been made difficult for you by the failures of earthly fathers. Then do not measure God by what has been missing. Let Him become the measure of what fatherhood truly is. Every kindness you longed for, every refuge you needed, every steadfast love your heart has searched for finds its source in Him. He is not the shadow cast by earthly fathers; they are but faint shadows of Him. And He will not rest until every one of His children has come home, not merely to His house, but to His very heart.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, shape my heart until it delights in Yours.*
