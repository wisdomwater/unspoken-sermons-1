## 69 - Learning Christ Himself

> *It is possible to know many things about Christ and still not truly know Him. The truth is not merely spoken by Jesus—it is lived in Jesus.*

The question is not simply whether we have learned about Christ, but whether we have learned Christ Himself. Many thoughts may fill the mind while the heart remains untouched. We may defend doctrines, repeat sacred words, and argue over theology, yet never come to share the very life of the Son. To learn Christ is something far deeper than mastering ideas. It is to have His Spirit quietly shaping our own, until we begin to see with His eyes, love with His heart, and trust the Father as He trusted Him.

Whenever an image of God awakens fear without love, cruelty without compassion, or power without goodness, we must bring that image into the light of Jesus Christ. The Son is not one voice among many speaking about the Father. He is the Father's perfect self-revelation. Whatever cannot live in the light of Christ's character cannot be the final truth about God. The One who welcomed sinners, healed the broken, forgave His enemies, and laid down His life for the world has forever shown us what the Father's heart is like.

This does not mean that right thinking is unimportant. Truth matters deeply because God Himself is truth. Yet the purpose of truth is not to win arguments but to transform lives. A humble soul walking faithfully with God, though burdened with many imperfect ideas, is nearer the kingdom than one whose theology is flawless but whose heart has never learned the obedience of love. Our real belief is not measured first by what we can explain, but by the life we actually live before God and our neighbor.

Therefore, let every cherished opinion remain beneath the lordship of Christ Himself. Follow no doctrine more faithfully than you follow the living Lord. Wherever He leads, have courage to go, even if old certainties must be left behind. The Father has not called us to cling to our ideas about Him, but to know Him. And whoever walks with the Son will find that truth is no cold proposition, but the warm and living presence of God transforming the soul from glory to glory.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Lord Jesus, teach me to know You, not merely to know about You.*
