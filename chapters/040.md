## 40 - Remember the Father

> *We do not fail to understand because God is unclear. We fail because anxiety fills the place where trust was meant to dwell.*

The disciples had seen bread multiplied in their own hands. They had gathered baskets overflowing with what remained. Yet when they found themselves with only one loaf, fear returned as though they had learned nothing. At the mention of leaven, their thoughts rushed immediately to bread. Their minds were so occupied with what they lacked that they could no longer hear what the Lord was truly saying.

Our Master did not first correct their misunderstanding. He reached deeper than the mistake itself. Misunderstandings are only the fruit; distrust is the root. Before teaching them the meaning of His words, He called them to remember what they had already seen. "How many baskets did you gather?" He was not testing their memory of numbers. He was awakening their memory of the Father's heart. They remembered the miracle, but they had forgotten the Love that performed it.

How often we do the same. We recall God's past mercies, yet still imagine ourselves abandoned when tomorrow seems uncertain. We know the stories of His faithfulness, but anxiety quietly persuades us that this time we must carry the burden alone. Fear narrows the soul until every word becomes about bread, every circumstance about survival. A heart crowded with care cannot easily receive the deeper things of God.

The miracles of Christ were never merely displays of power. They were windows into the Father's character. Every loaf broken declared that God knows what His children need before they ask. Every act of compassion proclaimed that love is not an occasional work of God, but His very nature. Jesus did not come chiefly to convince us that He possessed divine power, but to reveal that the Father is eternally this kind of Father—one whose care is nearer than our next breath.

When your heart begins to race toward tomorrow, remember the Father before you remember your need. The next hour belongs no more to you than the next century. Your calling is only to be faithful in the moment love has placed before you. The future is already held in the hands that multiplied the bread. As trust returns, understanding follows. The soul at rest begins once more to hear the voice of Christ clearly.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, quiet my fears and teach me to trust Your care.*
