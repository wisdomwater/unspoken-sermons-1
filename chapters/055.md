## 55 - More Life

> *Every true longing is, at its heart, a longing for more of the life that comes from God. The Father does not merely rescue us from death; He shares His own life with us.*

When Christ said, "I came that they may have life," He spoke of the deepest need of every heart. Beneath all our restless desires, beneath every disappointment and every fear, there is one great hunger: we need more life. We mistake this longing for countless other things, but the soul was made for nothing less than the life of God Himself. Only the Father can satisfy what the Father created.

Even those who despair of life are not truly seeking death. They long to escape the death already at work within them—the loneliness, weariness, fear, shame, and emptiness that have dimmed their joy. What they call life has become so overshadowed that they cannot imagine anything beyond it. Yet the answer has never been less life, but more. The Good Shepherd does not merely remove pain; He pours His own abundant life into the places where death has taken hold.

Nor is this longing ours alone. The whole creation waits with us. There is a quiet ache running through the world, as though every flower stretching toward the sun and every creature lifting its eyes carries an unspoken hope. All things are moving toward the day when the children of God will fully become what they were created to be, and the Father's life will shine through them without hindrance. Creation itself waits for that unveiling.

Why should such a work take so long? Because the Father desires more than creatures who obey by necessity. Love cannot be manufactured. He is bringing forth sons and daughters who freely choose His goodness because they have come to know His heart. Such freedom requires patience, and such patience is the labor of divine love. From the beginning, God has been giving Himself that we might one day willingly return ourselves to Him. His purpose is not merely that we should exist, but that we should share His own life in joyful union forever.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, fill me with Your abundant life.*
