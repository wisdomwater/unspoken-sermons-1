## 19 - Tempted by Lesser Goods

> *The deepest temptations are seldom invitations to obvious evil. They are invitations to choose a lesser good before the Father's perfect time.*

Many imagine that our Lord was tempted by things He could never have desired. But temptation cannot touch what the heart sees only as ugly. Christ was not drawn toward evil itself. He was tested by real goods presented in the wrong way, at the wrong time, apart from the Father's will. The enemy's subtle question was never, "Will you choose evil?" It was, "Will you settle for something less than the Father's perfect good?"

So it is with us. The most dangerous choices are rarely between darkness and light. They are between trusting God and grasping at what appears good enough. We long for security, for relief, for influence, for success, for comfort. None of these are evil. They become destructive only when we seize them instead of receiving them from the Father's hand. Every temptation whispers that we need not wait upon love, because we can secure life for ourselves.

Our Lord's hunger in the wilderness reveals this mystery. Hunger itself was no sin. Bread was no sin. To restore strength was good. Yet even this good could not become His master. The Son would not step outside His perfect trust in the Father, even to satisfy a true and pressing need. He chose not merely bread, but the deeper life from which bread itself receives its goodness. He would rather remain hungry than cease, even for a moment, to live from the Father's will.

This is the freedom the Father desires for His children. He does not ask us to despise His gifts but to love Him more deeply than His gifts. Then every blessing may be received with gratitude instead of fear, and every season of waiting becomes a quiet place where trust grows strong. The soul that rests in the Father's goodness can patiently refuse lesser goods, knowing that Love never withholds the greater.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, keep my heart faithful to Your highest good.*
