## 44 - A Better Answer

> *The truest answer to prayer is not always the gift we seek, but the God who gives Himself. Love answers every prayer according to eternity, not merely according to the moment.*

Many wonder whether God hears prayer because they cannot always see the answer they hoped for. Yet the deepest assurance is never found by argument, nor by the testimony of another. It is found only by coming to the Father yourself. Faith does not begin by proving that prayer works. It begins by bringing your need into the presence of the One who loves you. The child learns the Father's heart not by studying it from afar, but by living within His embrace.

When a prayer seems unanswered, we are tempted to believe we have been forgotten. But the Father's silence is never neglect. He does not weigh our requests against His convenience but against His perfect love. A wise father does not surrender the future of his child to the passing desires of a single hour. He sees farther than the child can see. He knows which gifts will heal, which will harm, and which must wait until the heart is ready to receive them.

Often the deepest prayer hidden beneath our words is answered, though the words themselves are not. We ask for relief, while God prepares strength. We ask for one path, while He leads us by another that reaches the truer destination. So it was with Paul, whose burden remained while Christ's grace became his greater possession. The Father's highest purpose is never merely to remove our weakness, but to draw us into His own life, where His strength is made perfect within us.

Why, then, does God ask us to pray if He already knows our needs? Because prayer is not meant merely to change our circumstances but to awaken our hearts. Need becomes the hand that leads the child home. Every true prayer opens the soul wider to receive the greatest gift of all: the Father Himself. His gifts are precious because they come from His love, but they are never meant to replace His presence. Every answer, whether it comes as "yes," "wait," or "not this," is an invitation into deeper communion with the One whose goodness never fails.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to trust the wisdom of Your love.*
