## 3 - The Childlike Heart of God

> *The highest glory of God is not that He exalts Himself above all, but that He gives Himself to all. The heart of a child does not diminish His majesty—it reveals it.*

If we could see the kingdom as heaven sees it, I believe we would behold the Lord with a child in His arms, surrounded by those who have learned, however imperfectly, to become like Him. In every disciple there would be the dawning light of childlikeness, but in the face of Christ it would shine without shadow. His wisdom has never displaced His trust. His greatness has never outgrown His humility. The Son stands before the Father with the joyful confidence of perfect love, and in that holy childhood we glimpse the very life of God.

Many imagine that childhood belongs only to our beginnings, something to be left behind as we mature. Yet when the eternal Son entered our world as a child, He did not merely pass through childhood on His way to something greater. He revealed that childlikeness belongs to the deepest reality of God's own life. The Son has always lived in loving trust toward the Father, and the Father has always delighted in pouring Himself out for the Son. Love flows between Them without grasping, without rivalry, without fear. This is the life into which we are invited.

Do not fear, then, that drawing near to God means approaching One who is chiefly concerned with His own honor. Such thoughts arise more easily from human kingdoms than from the kingdom of heaven. Our Father's greatness is not measured by how much He demands for Himself, but by how completely He gives Himself away. His joy is to bless. His glory is to restore. He seeks not servants who tremble at a distance, but children who learn to trust His heart.

As this vision settles within you, let it quiet every fear that God is reluctant, harsh, or self-seeking. He is infinitely stronger than you can imagine, yet His strength is the strength of love. He is higher than all creation, yet He stoops lower than any parent to lift a beloved child. His single purpose is that you should share His own life, until His love becomes your love, His joy your joy, and His childlike freedom your everlasting home.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to trust the goodness of Your heart.*
