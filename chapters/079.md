## 79 - Mirrors of the Lord

> *We become like the One upon whom we lovingly gaze. The heart that continually welcomes Christ is quietly transformed into His likeness.*

The Christian life is not first a matter of striving but of beholding. We enter the presence of Christ with unveiled hearts, no longer separated by fear or distance, and we learn simply to remain before Him. As Moses reflected a passing glory after standing in God's presence, so we receive a greater glory by living in the presence of the Son. Yet this glory is not borrowed for a moment—it is meant to become the very life within us.

The heart is like a living mirror. Whatever it welcomes most deeply begins to shape it. When we continually dwell upon fear, resentment, or pride, those things slowly carve their likeness into us. But when we turn again and again toward Jesus—His compassion, His humility, His trust in the Father—His image begins to fill the depths of our being. We do not merely think about Him; we make room for Him.

This transformation is not mechanical. Christ is not an idea that we imitate from a distance. Whenever we truly open our hearts to Him, He Himself enters by His Spirit. The One we behold is the One who quietly works within us. As sunlight warms a window simply because it stands open, so the soul is changed by remaining open before the living Christ. The work is His, yet our willing openness is the door through which He comes.

So do not measure your growth only by dramatic moments or outward achievements. Ask instead whether your heart returns more readily to Christ than it once did. Are you becoming gentler, freer, more patient, more truthful? Then His glory is already becoming your glory. Little by little, almost unnoticed, the Mirror fills with His face until, one day, others catch a glimpse of Jesus when they look upon your life.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Lord Jesus, fill the mirror of my heart with the beauty of Your presence.*
