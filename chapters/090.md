## 90 - Made One with God

> *Christ did not come to make the Father willing to receive us. He came to make us willing and able to return to the Father.*

I believe in Jesus Christ, the eternal Son of God, my elder Brother, Lord, and Master. I believe He has the right to my obedience wherever I know His will, and that to obey Him is not to become less myself, but to rise toward the fullness of my being. He died that I might die as He died—to every rule within me except the will of the Father—and live with a heart ready to say, “Do with me as You will.”

Jesus saves me from myself: from all that has grown out of self-love, fear, pretence, unfairness, cowardice, anxiety, and the desire to possess. He saves me not by hiding what I am beneath a borrowed righteousness, but by making me righteous. He delivers me into the glad freedom of a child who loves what is lovely and desires nothing he would be ashamed for heaven and earth to see. He teaches me that God is just like Jesus—only greater still, the beauty from which all beauty flows.

This is the atonement: not the Father reconciled to us, but we reconciled to the Father. God has never ceased to be our Father. Christ entered the gulf created by our fear and selfishness, stood before us as the true image of God, and held that witness even unto death. All the waves of human evil broke upon Him, yet He returned no evil. He received hatred and answered with love, received death and rose with life. Thus He opened the way for the Father’s life to enter ours.

Jesus is not only the One who brings us back to God; He also teaches us to make peace with one another. Under His living influence, repentance becomes more than sorrow. It becomes confession, restitution, renewed love, and faithful service. Have I injured another? Christ leads me to make amends. Have I failed to love? He gives me a better love than I had before. He is our atonement because He makes us at one with God and therefore capable of becoming one with our brothers and sisters.

Do not imagine the Father as a ruler who laid aside His fatherhood in order to become a judge. His right over us comes precisely from His being our Father. His justice is the determination of love to make us clean, whole, and true. He will forgive without measure, but He will not call darkness light or leave us imprisoned in what destroys us. In Jesus we see the God who is light, and in Him there is no darkness at all.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Jesus, make me one with the Father.*
