## 89 - Faith Is Obedience

> *Your theory about Christ is not your faith in Christ. Faith begins when you trust Him enough to do what He says.*

Many souls have been troubled by teachings that make the Father appear less righteous than the best goodness they know. They have been told that God could forgive only after the innocent suffered in place of the guilty, as though the Father required pain before love could flow. But Jesus did not come to shelter us from an unwilling Father. He came to reveal the Father—to show us the One whose justice and mercy are eternally one.

God will never call evil good, pretend a person is righteous while leaving them unchanged, or overlook the smallest thing that is destroying His child. He will excuse every weakness that truth permits Him to excuse. He will cherish every spark of goodness. He will not break the bruised reed or extinguish the smoking flax. Yet He will not cease working until His child is clean, true, and free. This is not severity opposed to love. It is the steadfastness of perfect fatherhood.

Do not despise those who have held mistaken ideas of God. Many who believed such theories were far better than their theories. They knew the Father through obedience to Christ, even while their minds carried teachings their hearts had never fully examined. We may honor the goodness in a person without calling every belief good. A lie does not become truth because a holy person once repeated it; nor does a mistaken belief erase the holiness formed through faithful obedience.

But what theory should replace the old one? None is needed. Do not exchange one system for another and imagine that you have found life. Your faith is not the explanation you give of Christ’s work. Your faith is the trust by which you follow Him. Feed the hungry. Forgive your enemy. Speak truth. Refuse retaliation. Make peace. Love your neighbor. Do the part of His will you already understand, and the Spirit of the Master will teach you what you are ready to know.

Theory may grow from a life of obedience, but life cannot grow from theory alone. You cannot postpone following Jesus until every mystery has been solved. That is like refusing the medicine until you have mastered the whole science of healing. Take the next step He has shown you. Truth becomes visible to the one willing to become true. As you walk with Christ, your thoughts of God will slowly take the shape of the Father revealed in Him.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Jesus, teach me truth through obedience.*
