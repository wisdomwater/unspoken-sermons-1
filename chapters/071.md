## 71 - Begin with the Next Step

> *Faith grows not by endless examination of itself, but by the next act of loving obedience. The smallest "yes" to Christ opens the heart to His life.*

Many ask, "How do I awaken? How do I begin?" The answer is wonderfully simple. Rise, and do the next thing your Master has already asked of you. Do not wait until your faith feels stronger or your understanding becomes clearer. Obedience is not the reward of mature faith; it is the path by which faith matures. The child learns to trust the Father's hand by taking hold of it.

Spend less time asking whether you believe enough, and more time asking whether you have listened today. Have you forgiven where Christ told you to forgive? Have you shown mercy where He called you to show mercy? Have you sought first the kingdom? Have you released an anxious care into the Father's keeping? The Lord's commands are not burdens placed upon unwilling servants. They are invitations into His own way of living. Each act of obedience draws us deeper into fellowship with Him.

Do not be discouraged because your obedience is imperfect. The Father is not waiting for flawless performance before He receives you. He delights in the willing heart that begins. Christ never commands what He does not also intend to accomplish within those who follow Him. As we offer our imperfect "yes," He patiently shapes our desires until obedience becomes joy and His will becomes our own.

This is the secret of salvation: not merely believing that God has acted, but living in union with the God who is acting still. Faith is the heart resting in the Father's goodness and responding to His voice. As we walk with the Son, trust deepens, fear loosens its hold, and the life of God quietly fills every corner of our being. In the end, salvation is nothing less than God and His child becoming of one heart and one will in love.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, give me grace to obey the next word You speak.*
