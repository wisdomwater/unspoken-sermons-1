## 99 - The Victory of Truth

> *Truth does not conquer by destroying the sinner, but by awakening him. Its final triumph is the moment a false heart sees itself clearly and turns homeward to the Father.*

The most terrible unveiling is not that others should see what we are, but that we ourselves should see it. A person may condemn dishonesty, cruelty, greed, or betrayal in others while carrying the same seed secretly within. We recognize evil easily when it wears a stranger’s face; it is harder to recognize when it has learned our voice, borrowed our reasons, and hidden beneath our virtues.

This is why we must be humble in judgment. Judas was not born a traitor unlike the rest of humanity. Treachery grew in him through small refusals of truth until even his affection became capable of betrayal. The same dark seed may lie in any heart that does not learn to love the neighbour as itself. Therefore let us tremble less for others than for our own hidden falseness, and hope for every person, knowing that none is beyond the Father’s awakening light.

The punishment of evil is to see evil as it truly is. No pain imposed from without could equal the anguish of awakening to the harm one has freely done. Yet this very vision is the beginning of salvation. The moment the sinner truly sees and hates the falsehood within him, he has already begun to turn toward righteousness. Judgment and mercy meet there: the deepest shame opens directly into the possibility of forgiveness.

Revenge can never be satisfied in the Father’s universe. When the one who injured us finally sees the wound he caused, he will grieve more deeply than our anger could ever demand. In that hour, if we share the heart of Christ, pity will rise where vengeance once stood. The returning wrongdoer will no longer appear merely as an enemy, but as a brother who was lost and has begun to come alive.

Let us begin forgiving before that hour arrives. Let us refuse to build our identity around the guilt of another. The final unveiling will reveal hidden evil, but it will also uncover hidden goodness, misunderstood motives, forgotten sacrifices, and faces more beautiful than we imagined. Whatever the truth reveals, the child of God may welcome it, for every unmasking brings creation nearer to the kingdom of light.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let truth conquer me through mercy.*
