## 57 - The Life That Chooses Love

> *Eternal life is not merely living forever. It is awakening into the life of God, where love becomes the deepest desire and obedience becomes perfect freedom.*

The life Christ offers is far more than endless existence. We already exist, yet we often know how little alive we truly are. Eternal life is the life of God shared with His children. It is a heart awakened to righteousness, truth, and love. It is the soul becoming so united with the Father that His goodness is no longer merely admired but lived. This is the life for which you were created.

How, then, does such life grow within us? Not by striving to manufacture holiness, but by bringing our will into harmony with the Father's. Deep within every person lies the power to answer His invitation. Whenever we sincerely pray, "Not my will, but Yours be done," something eternal is joined together. The Father's creating will meets the willing obedience of His child, and divine life flows where resistance once stood. Obedience is not the loss of ourselves; it is the finding of our truest self within the Father's heart.

As this life grows, love becomes our native language. We cease living for ourselves and begin discovering that every person is a revelation of God's image. Our neighbor is no longer an obstacle or a rival but a sacred gift through whom we learn the heart of the Father. We were never made to revolve around ourselves. Self shrivels the soul, but love enlarges it until there seems no end to the life it can contain.

The Father desires to make your soul an ever-expanding vessel for His own life. Every act of trust widens its capacity. Every surrender makes room for greater joy. What seems impossible now will one day become as natural as breathing, for the love of God is endlessly creative. We cannot yet imagine the fullness that awaits us, but we may already begin to taste it whenever we choose love over self, truth over illusion, and the Father's will over our own. There is no richer life than this, and there never will be.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your will become my deepest joy.*
