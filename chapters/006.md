## 6 - The Fire That Will Not Let Go

> *God does not destroy the person He created. His fire destroys only what keeps that person from becoming fully alive.*

Even Moses, who spoke with God as few have ever done, could not yet behold the fullness of the Father's heart. The revelation was not withheld because God was unwilling to give it, but because no one could yet bear to see such love. Only when Christ came did we behold the face that had always been hidden—the face of the Son who would rather give Himself than demand another's sacrifice. In Him we learn that God has never sought satisfaction through suffering, but restoration through love.

This is the wonder of divine love: it does not ask whether we deserve to be saved before it begins its work. It simply refuses to abandon what it has made. We may resist it, misunderstand it, even fear it, but love does not surrender its purpose. It burns because it must. Not because God delights in pain, but because He delights in wholeness. Whatever keeps us from becoming His true children cannot remain forever in the presence of His holy fire.

So do not mistake God's judgment for His rejection. What His fire consumes is never the person He imagined from the beginning. It is the fearful, grasping, self-made life that we mistake for ourselves. Pride, bitterness, greed, vanity, and every false refuge must one day pass away. Yet beneath them all lives the true self—the son, the daughter—fashioned in the image of God and kept alive by His unceasing love. When the fire has finished its work, that hidden life will stand forth with a freedom and joy it has never known.

Even the darkest places of the soul are not beyond the reach of the Father's purpose. To be without the felt comfort of God is a sorrow deeper than words can tell, yet His hand never fully lets go of those He has made. Love pursues where nothing else can follow. It searches every shadow, burns through every illusion, and patiently waits until the heart, weary of its loneliness, turns again toward home. The consuming fire is still the fire of love.

Therefore let your heart welcome the Father's purifying work, even when it is costly. What He removes, He removes because He loves. What He preserves, He preserves forever. One day every false thing will have vanished, death itself will be no more, and all that His love has created will shine with the beauty He always intended. Then we shall discover that His fire was never the enemy of our lives. It was the faithful servant of His mercy, carrying us at last into the joy of being fully His.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your holy love make me truly myself.*
