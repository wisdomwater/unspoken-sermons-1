## 93 - The Anger of Love

> *There is an anger that grows from hatred, and there is an anger that rises because love refuses to abandon the beloved. The displeasure of Jesus is never against us as His creatures, but against all that keeps us from becoming true.*

We are sometimes troubled by the thought that Jesus could be angry. We imagine anger only as we have too often known it—in wounded pride, impatience, contempt, or the desire to strike back. But the anger of Jesus is nothing like this. His anger belongs wholly to His love. It is the sorrow and indignation of One who sees a child refusing the very life offered to him.

The leper was healed, yet would not obey. The blind men received their sight, yet preferred their own idea of gratitude to the Lord’s command. They wished to honor Jesus, but they would not listen to Him. They offered noise instead of obedience, praise instead of friendship. They wanted to repay His gift in the manner they thought best, without asking what He desired.

This is still one of our deepest temptations. We may speak much for Christ, defend His name, labor for His cause, and yet resist the quiet thing He has asked of us. We invent great services while neglecting simple obedience. We would build Him a throne, though He has asked us to follow Him on the road. We may even hinder His work by trying to serve Him according to our own wisdom.

His displeasure, then, is not proof that His love has withdrawn. It is proof that He takes us seriously. He is angry because we are capable of more, because we were made to know the Father’s will and gladly share in it. He will not treat our disobedience as though it does not matter, for our freedom and the healing of others may depend upon our becoming true.

Yet even in anger, Jesus does not seek revenge. He did not return the leper to his disease or take sight again from the men who disobeyed Him. His anger remained the anger of a healer. Whether He gives or withholds, corrects or waits, He is always working toward deliverance. Blessed is the soul that comes near enough to Christ to discover that even His displeasure is love—and that His love will not rest until our obedience becomes joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Jesus, let even Your correction lead me nearer to Your heart.*
