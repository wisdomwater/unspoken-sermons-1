## 41 - The Freedom of Trust

> *The heart is never enslaved by things alone, but by the fear of losing them or the longing to possess them. The Father invites His children into a freedom where He Himself is their treasure.*

How easily the smallest care can fill the whole horizon of the soul. One person is hindered by abundance, another by want, yet both stumble over the same stone: neither has learned to rest in the Father's goodness. The rich man clings to what he has, fearing to let it go. The anxious disciple clings to what he lacks, fearing it will never come. Whether our hands are full or empty, it is distrust that closes our hearts to God. The Father would have us find our security, not in His gifts, but in Him.

Our Lord never lived as though tomorrow ruled today. He gave Himself wholly to the work the Father placed before Him, trusting that every need would be met in its proper hour. Such trust is not carelessness but the deepest wisdom. Every unnecessary worry steals strength from the present moment and blinds us to the quiet work of God already unfolding around us. The Father is never absent from the life of His child, though His hand is often hidden from our sight.

When God withholds something we think we need, He has not ceased to be good. He is not taking away to leave us empty but making room for a greater gift. Often we ask for relief while He desires to give us Himself. We ask for certainty while He offers trust. We ask for comfort while He invites us into communion. No loving father delights in denying his child, yet every wise father knows there are gifts that can only be received through waiting, surrender, and faith.

The soul grows free as it loosens its grip on every possession, every fear, and every demand that life unfold according to its own design. Then the heart becomes like a clear stream instead of a stagnant pool, receiving from God and gladly giving away. Such a soul discovers that heaven is not first a place but the life of God's children, content in His presence and rejoicing in one another. There, love has driven out both greed and fear, and the Father's joy has become their own.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach my heart to rest in Your goodness.*
