## 25 - Faith Beyond Feeling

> *The deepest faith is not the warmth of devotion but the steadfastness of the will that still turns toward the Father when every light has gone out.*

There are seasons when God seems wonderfully near. Prayer flows easily, hope rises naturally, and love fills the heart without effort. We receive these moments with gratitude, for they are gifts of the Father's kindness. Yet He does not intend that our lives should rest upon feelings alone. There comes a time when the warmth fades, not because He has ceased to love us, but because He is teaching us to love Him with a freedom that is truly our own.

The Father does not hold His children so tightly that they never learn to walk. In His wisdom, He sometimes steps back—not to abandon us, but to awaken our own willing trust. He gives us room to choose Him. In those hidden hours, when no holy impulse seems to stir within us, the quiet decision to turn again toward God becomes precious beyond measure. The will that whispers, "You are still my Father," even without comfort, is being shaped into His likeness.

Do not be troubled if your heart feels dull or your prayers seem empty. You are not required to manufacture holy feelings. You are only called to remain faithful. When love feels absent, choose what love would do. When hope seems lost, do the next good thing before you. Offer forgiveness. Keep your promise. Sweep the floor. Prepare a meal. Visit the lonely. Small acts of obedience become quiet declarations that darkness will not have the final word.

If the day should come when even the name of God seems distant, remember the cry of Christ upon the cross. He did not wait until He could feel the Father before He said, *My God.* He spoke those words from the depths of desolation, and they became His victory. So may they become yours. Hold fast, however weakly. The Father sees you even when you cannot see Him, and the One who began His work within you will not rest until your freedom and His love have become one.

**Breath Prayer**

> *My Father, when I cannot feel You, help me to choose You still.*
