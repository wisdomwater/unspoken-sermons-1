## Day 80 - When Christ Becomes Life in Us

> *Christ does not enter the heart merely to comfort or instruct it. He comes to reshape the soul until His truth, freedom, and love become our own.*

When we open the heart to Christ, we receive more than an image of goodness. We receive the living Lord Himself. He enters the deepest place within us, not as a stranger imposing something foreign, but as the One from whom our very life first came. His presence does not destroy the self; it awakens the self into what the Father always intended it to become.

This is the glory of salvation: Christ makes us like Himself through communion. He works within our thoughts, desires, choices, and affections until goodness is no longer merely commanded from outside but rises freely from within. He teaches us to love the Father, to care for our neighbour, to welcome truth, and to seek nothing above the will of God. Where His Spirit dwells, there is no violence against the soul. There is liberty.

To receive Christ is therefore to receive the Truth. Truth is not merely the accuracy of a statement or the discovery of a fact. A person may know many facts and yet remain false at heart. Truth is deeper. It is the harmony between what we are, what we say, and what God created us to become. Jesus could say, “I am the truth,” because there was no division in Him. His whole being answered perfectly to the Father.

The created world also whispers this truth. The sky, the light, the solidity of earth, and the unfolding of a flower reveal something of the mind and character of God. Their deepest meaning is not found merely by taking them apart, but by seeing what they express of their Maker. Creation is not only matter governed by laws; it is the garment of divine thought, love made visible to the childlike heart.

So let Christ dwell within you. Hold His face in the mirror of your heart until His life becomes your life. As you become true in Him, you will begin to see truth everywhere—in conscience, in creation, in love, and in the Father whose glory shines through all things. The One who made you will not cease His work until your life answers freely and joyfully to His own.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Lord Jesus, make my whole being true in You.*
