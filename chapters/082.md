## 82 - The Blossom of Sonship

> *The deepest truth about you is not what you have done, but whose child you are becoming. Freedom blossoms wherever the Father's life is welcomed and His love is obeyed.*

There is a longing hidden within every heart that nothing in this world can quiet. Even if you were faithful in every duty toward your neighbor, even if your life were honorable and kind, there would still remain a holy hunger. It is the longing of a child for the Father. A child may not yet know his father's face, yet he feels the absence of the One for whom he was made. So it is with every soul. We were created not merely to do what is right, but to live in joyful fellowship with the God from whom our very being came.

When at last you become aware that you cannot heal yourself, that there is within you both a love for the good and a resistance to it, you have not wandered away from truth—you have entered it. The struggle itself is evidence that something deeper is calling you home. Do not despise the parts of yourself that seem wild or untamed. The Father does not seek to destroy what He has made, but to bring every part of your nature into harmony beneath the gentle rule of His love. Wherever God truly reigns, even strength that once wounded becomes strength that serves.

The soul begins to blossom when it entrusts itself to the Father. Imagine the peace of a little child walking hand in hand with one who knows every step of the journey. The child need not understand the road because he trusts the one who leads him. So it is with the children of God. As we surrender ourselves into the care of the Father who knows us better than we know ourselves, fear begins to loosen its grip, and freedom quietly takes root within us. Truth is no longer merely something we believe; it becomes the life we live.

All of this has been perfectly revealed in Jesus Christ. He is not simply one who taught the truth—He is the living Truth because every moment of His life was a perfect "yes" to the Father. His obedience was not reluctant submission but the joyful flowering of divine sonship. And this same life is at work within you. Christ is the hidden root from which your own true self grows. The person God dreamed when He created you is nothing less than the life of Christ coming to full bloom in your unique humanity.

Do not lose heart because the blossom is slow to open. The Father Himself tends the garden of your soul. He gives you the desire to seek Him, the strength to choose Him, and the grace to keep walking when your own strength fails. Your willing matters, for love never coerces; yet even your willingness is awakened by His first love. One day every falsehood will fall away, and you will stand fully alive, fully free, perfectly yourself because you are perfectly His. To be one with the Father in Christ is the joy for which you were created, and no lesser destiny will satisfy either your heart or His.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Christ blossom fully within me.*
