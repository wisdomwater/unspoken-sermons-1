## 35 - The Way into Life

> *God does not seek perfect performance before He welcomes you. He awakens in you a hunger that only His own life can satisfy.*

Our Lord did not come merely to make people behave better. He came to awaken a deeper longing—the longing to become truly good. There is a great difference between doing the right thing because one must and loving what is right because one's heart has been made alive. The Father desires children whose obedience flows as naturally as fruit from a healthy tree. Such righteousness cannot be manufactured; it is the life of God growing within us.

Yet every journey must begin somewhere. When the young man asked how to enter life, Jesus did not dismiss the commandments. He sent him back to what he already knew. *Keep them.* Not because obedience itself is the fullness of life, but because it is the road that leads toward it. The commandments are not the destination. They are the path that teaches us our need for the One who alone can fulfill them within us.

Do not imagine that your Father despises every imperfect attempt to obey. No loving father scorns the unsteady steps of a child learning to walk. He rejoices in them, even while calling the child onward toward maturity. God is not satisfied with anything less than perfection, because He desires nothing less than your complete restoration. Yet He delights in every sincere movement toward Him, for each small act of faith prepares the heart to receive a greater measure of His own life.

So it was with the young ruler. His efforts had not made him perfect, but neither had they been wasted. They had accomplished their true purpose: they left him hungry. He had discovered that no amount of obedience could quiet the longing within him. The law had brought him to the threshold where grace could meet him. Blessed is the soul that finds itself there, no longer trusting in what it has done, but yearning for the life only God can give.

Then Jesus revealed the next step. "Sell what you have...and come, follow Me." The issue was never wealth alone. It was freedom. Whatever we cannot surrender has quietly become our master. The Father never asks us to loosen our grip on lesser things because He delights in loss. He asks because He longs to place into our empty hands something infinitely greater—His own life. Every surrender is an invitation to become more fully His child, more fully alive, more fully free.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, free my heart to follow wherever Your love leads.*
