## 74 - The Life That Was in Him

> *The deepest life of Christ was not found in His power, but in His perfect love for the Father. His life became light because His whole being was one with God's will.*

The Son through whom all things were made possessed a greatness beyond creation itself. Before He ever called worlds into being, there was within Him a life unlike any other—the life of perfect love. His glory was not first that He could create, but that He delighted without reserve to do the Father's will. The eternal heartbeat of the Son was this joyful response: "Not My will, but Yours." In that perfect surrender, divine life shone without shadow.

Love has always been the deepest truth of God. Power alone could never make Him worthy of worship. If God ruled only by strength, we might fear Him, but we could never love Him. Yet the Father is love, and everything He creates springs from that inexhaustible fountain. Creation itself is the overflow of His goodness. The Son reveals that this same love is not only the source of life—it is the very life itself.

This is why Christ calls us to deny ourselves. Self-denial is not the destruction of our humanity but its awakening. As long as we live for ourselves, our hearts remain restless and divided. But whenever we yield our will to the Father, life begins to flow through us again. Like a child reconciled to a loving parent, the soul relaxes, light returns to the eyes, and joy quietly rises from within. Obedience is not slavery; it is the restoration of our true nature.

The life of Jesus is offered to us as our own. He does not merely command us to follow His example; He shares His very relationship with the Father. As we choose, again and again, to let love govern self-interest, His life awakens within us. The light that shone in Christ begins to shine in His brothers and sisters, until we too discover that our deepest freedom is found, not in asserting ourselves, but in joyfully belonging to the Father.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let the life of Your Son become the life of my heart.*
