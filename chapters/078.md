## 78 - Recognizing the Son

> *We recognize Christ, not because we have mastered every doctrine, but because our hearts have learned to love what He loves.*

No one has seen God with earthly eyes. Yet every heart that longs for truth has glimpsed something of Him. A quiet whisper of conscience, an unexpected mercy, a longing for goodness, a beauty that stirs the soul—these are traces of the Father's presence. Wherever a person sincerely welcomes that light, however faint, the heart is being prepared to recognize the Son.

Jesus taught that those who truly knew the Father would know Him when He appeared. Why? Because love recognizes love. The one who has learned to delight in righteousness instinctively turns toward the One in whom righteousness is perfectly revealed. Christ does not need to force Himself upon such a heart. There is already a family resemblance. The soul whispers, "This is my home."

It is possible to know many things about Christ and still fail to recognize Him. Pride, fear, and cherished traditions can become veils over the eyes. Yet it is equally possible for a humble heart, even one burdened with many misunderstandings, to be ready for His voice. The Father is not looking first for flawless theology but for children who hunger to become true. Wherever that desire lives, Christ is already drawing near.

So let us not ask first whether we would recognize Jesus by His appearance, His clothing, or even by our expectations. Let us ask whether our hearts have become like His. Do we love mercy? Do we hunger for justice? Do we forgive as we have been forgiven? Do we delight in the Father's goodness? For those who love what Christ loves will one day find, to their great joy, that they have known His voice all along. As we behold His glory, we ourselves are slowly transformed into His likeness.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Lord Jesus, shape my heart until it gladly recognizes Yours.*
