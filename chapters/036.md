## 36 - Barefoot into Life

> *The command of Christ is never premature; it comes when the soul is ready either to obey or to discover what still holds it captive.*

Was the Lord asking too much of the young man? No. Love does not demand what the soul is incapable of giving. Jesus showed him the next step because the time had come for him to take it. Had he obeyed, freedom would have been born within him through the very act of obedience. Since he refused, another kind of knowledge began: he discovered the power his possessions held over him. There comes a moment when each of us must either follow the light we have been given or turn away and learn, through sorrow, what our refusal has revealed.

Yet do not imagine that the young man’s refusal closed the door forever. I have not learned such a Christ. He went away sorrowful, and sorrow is not always the end of hope. The word of Jesus had entered him and would not easily be silenced. Perhaps he later saw the Master Himself living without wealth, refusing earthly power, and surrendering everything to the Father. Perhaps one day he returned, laid down what he could not release before, and joined those who had discovered that the patience of God is greater than the weakness of man.

His riches seemed to give him weight, strength, and standing, but they were the very things keeping him near the ground. He thought them power, yet they had become a chain. He might gladly have used his wealth for Christ, but he could not yet let Christ free him from it. He was willing to devote what he possessed, but not to devote himself. He could not become naked as a child and allow the Father alone to be his security.

Obedience would have opened his eyes. We often wish to understand before we follow, but the understanding hidden in Christ’s commands is frequently found only after the step is taken. The young man stood upon the stair of life with the next step veiled in cloud. Then the Son of Man came near and showed him where to place his foot. But the young man wore golden shoes, and they were too heavy for the ascent. He could not climb farther unless he removed them.

So it is with everything we cannot surrender. The Father does not ask us to cast it away because He delights in stripping us bare. He calls us to walk barefoot because only the free can climb into freedom. Whatever must be left behind will seem poor enough once we have seen the face of Christ. And even when we turn away sorrowful, His look follows us, His word remains within us, and His love continues its patient work until we are ready to rise.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, free me to walk barefoot into Your life.*
