## 13 - The Light We Choose

> *The greatest danger is not that God will cease to seek us, but that we will cease to welcome His light. Yet even then, Love does not surrender its children.*

The Holy Spirit is the quiet witness of truth within us. He does not force Himself upon the heart but gently reveals what is real, what is good, and what is of God. When we welcome His light, forgiveness becomes something we experience from within. We know ourselves loved because His Spirit awakens our own spirit to answer, "Yes, this is my Father." But when a person knowingly turns against the truth, calling light darkness and darkness light, the very doorway through which forgiveness enters is closed.

This is why our Lord spoke so solemnly of the sin against the Holy Spirit. It is not that the Father grows unwilling to forgive, but that the heart has become unwilling to receive what forgiveness would bring. Such a soul has begun to love falsehood because falsehood protects the kingdom of self. Yet even this fearful warning is itself an act of mercy. Christ speaks these words not to drive sinners away, but to awaken them before they become content with the darkness.

Still, I cannot believe that darkness is stronger than the Father who made the soul. If there is breath within a person, then God has not abandoned His work. There may yet come an earthquake deep within the heart, a shaking that breaks apart every refuge of pride. The fire of God burns not to destroy the person but to consume all that keeps the person from life. Who can measure how patient that holy love may be, or where its restoring work may finally lead?

Look upon our Lord as He hangs upon the cross, praying for those who nailed Him there: "Father, forgive them." See Him receive even Judas with sorrow rather than hatred. If such mercy lives in the heart of Christ, shall we imagine it has limits narrower than our hope? No. The Father's purpose is always restoration. There are dreadful roads a soul may choose, and fearful darkness that may follow, but the end toward which divine love labors is that every child should one day stand in the light with a new name, known only between the Father and the beloved soul.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, keep my heart open to Your living truth.*
