## 26 - Into the Father's Hands

> *The safest place for the human spirit is the hands from which it first came. Every act of trust is simply returning ourselves to the Father who has never ceased holding us.*

The final words of Jesus were not merely spoken at the end of His life—they revealed the pattern by which He had lived every day. "Father, into Your hands I commend My spirit." From the quiet hours of prayer before dawn to His compassion for the weary and broken, His whole life had been a continual offering of Himself into the Father's care. His last breath was the completion of a lifelong trust.

This is the wonder of our own lives as well. We need not reserve these words for our dying day. They belong to every morning, every difficult conversation, every act of service, every burden we cannot carry, and every fear we cannot quiet. Whenever we entrust ourselves to the Father, we are simply returning His gift of life into the hands from which it came. There is no safer place for the soul than the love that first called it into being.

Do not imagine that you must first become worthy before you come. A loving father does not turn away the child who comes ashamed, frightened, or confused. He draws the child nearer. So our heavenly Father welcomes us most tenderly when we come aware of our weakness. If your heart is restless, place it in His hands. If your thoughts are dark, place them in His hands. If you have failed, place even your failure in His hands. His love is never exhausted by our need.

And as we learn to rest there ourselves, we begin to entrust others to the same faithful care. We discover that the Father who holds us also holds every one of our brothers and sisters. Our hearts widen until we long for all people to know the embrace that has found us. For we cannot truly rejoice that God is *my* Father without rejoicing that He is *our* Father. In that shared love, the peace of Christ becomes our own, and we learn, little by little, to live every day with open hands.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, I place my spirit into Your loving hands.*
