## 27 - Love Is Greater Than Law

> *The law may restrain the hands, but only love can transform the heart. God does not seek mere obedience; He desires children who share His own love.*

When our Lord gathered the whole law into the command to love our neighbor, He was not giving us a heavier burden. He was revealing the very heart from which every true command has always flowed. Love did not grow out of the law; the law grew out of love. Long before God ever said, "You shall not," His Father's heart was already seeking the good of His children.

It is possible to keep rules while the heart remains cold. We may refrain from obvious wrong and yet fail to truly see the person before us. Love asks for something deeper. It does not merely avoid harming another; it delights in their good. It does not calculate what justice requires but freely gives what kindness desires. Where love reigns, the law has already found its fulfillment, for love gladly does far more than duty could ever demand.

This is why the story of the Good Samaritan continues to speak with such quiet power. The Samaritan did not ask whether the wounded man belonged to his people or deserved his help. Compassion recognized kinship before the mind could invent reasons not to care. Every person whose need touches our life becomes our neighbor, and every act of mercy becomes an opportunity to share in the Father's own heart.

Do not be discouraged if you discover that love does not come easily. The law itself teaches us that we cannot become loving through effort alone. It awakens our hunger for something greater—for the very life of God within us. The Father is not merely teaching us to behave differently; He is patiently shaping us until His own love becomes the life by which we live. As we draw near to Christ, the heart slowly learns what no command alone could ever produce.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, fill my heart with the love that fulfills every law.*
