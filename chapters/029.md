## 29 - Love Learns by Loving

> *Love is not first understood and then practiced. It is practiced, and in the practicing, the heart awakens to the truth it could never reason its way into.*

We often wait until we feel love before we act in love. Yet the Father's wisdom leads us another way. There are truths too deep to be grasped by argument alone. They must be lived before they can be fully seen. The heart learns what the mind cannot discover by itself. A single act of mercy often teaches more than a hundred careful explanations.

This is why our Lord spoke of the Samaritan rather than debating the meaning of love. The man simply stopped, cared, lifted, and healed. Perhaps he began from duty more than delight. Yet in doing what love required, he drew nearer to love itself. Every obedient act opens the soul a little wider to the life of God. We become capable of loving because we have chosen, however imperfectly, to walk in love.

The Father has woven this lesson into the whole fabric of our lives. Family, friendship, and even the ordinary joys and conflicts of daily living are not accidents of existence. They are the quiet schoolrooms where He teaches our hearts to recognize one another as His beloved children. The affection we first learn for those nearest us is not meant to remain enclosed there. It is meant to widen until no one stands outside its embrace.

For every person bears the image of the same Father. The bond that unites us is deeper than shared blood, shared history, or shared affection. It is the life of God Himself, calling each of us home. As His love grows within us, we discover that the circle of neighbor continues to expand until it includes even those we once thought impossible to love. Such is the Father's purpose: not merely that we become kinder people, but that we learn to love with His own heart.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach my heart to grow wider with Your love.*
