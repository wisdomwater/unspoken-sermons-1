## 30 - The Wide Heart of the Father

> *The nearer we draw to the Father, the wider our love becomes. His heart has room for every one He has made, and He longs to enlarge ours until it resembles His.*

The love of our neighbor does not end with those who are easy to love. It begins in the home, grows among friends, and then quietly widens until every person we meet becomes someone entrusted to us by the Father. The stranger, the laborer, the one who serves us, the one who disappoints us, the one whose life has been disfigured by sin or sorrow—all are our neighbors. We do not choose them. God does. Our task is simply to receive them as they come.

As this love grows, we begin to see beneath appearances. The weary face on the street, the impatient voice across the counter, the hardened heart that seems closed to goodness—all conceal a son or daughter fashioned by the same Father's love. However dim the image has become, it has not been erased. The Father's gaze never stops at what a person has become; it always beholds what they were created to be.

This is why Christ never allows us to draw a circle around those worthy of our compassion. His own heart reached toward every lost sheep, and He would not rest until each was found. The love of God does not rejoice in exclusion but in restoration. If the Father continues seeking, how can His children cease hoping? The farther His mercy reaches, the more clearly we begin to understand His glory.

In the end, love is also our own freedom. So long as we live only for ourselves, we remain confined within the narrow walls of self-concern. But every time we step outside ourselves in love, we breathe the larger air of the kingdom. There we discover that life is not found by turning inward, but by sharing in the Father's own generous heart. To love another is already to begin living the life of heaven.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, enlarge my heart until it reflects Your love for all.*
