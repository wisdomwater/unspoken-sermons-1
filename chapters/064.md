## 64 - When God Is the Answer

> *The greatest answer God gives is not an explanation but His presence. When we truly behold Him, trust begins where certainty ends.*

We often believe that peace will come when every question has been answered. Yet the Father knows that our deepest need is not information but Himself. Job longed for an explanation of his suffering, but when God finally spoke, He did not untangle every mystery. Instead, He unveiled His own greatness. He lifted Job's eyes from the narrow circle of his pain to the vast wisdom woven through all creation. The One who guides the stars, feeds the wild creatures, and orders the seasons can surely be trusted with the story of one human soul.

The child of God does not trust because every path is visible, but because the Father's heart has become known. There are countless things beyond our understanding, yet none of them lie beyond His. Every mystery is not a wall meant to keep us from Him but a door inviting us into deeper confidence. What we cannot yet comprehend is not chaos but wisdom waiting for its appointed time. If God knows, then hope remains. The unknown is never empty when it is held within the hands of Love.

For us, the Father's face shines even more clearly than it did for Job. We have seen Him in Jesus Christ. When troubling questions arise—about suffering, loss, injustice, or the hidden workings of providence—we need not imagine a God unlike the Son. Christ has forever interpreted the Father. Whatever remains mysterious, this is settled: the One who stretched out His hands upon the cross cannot be less loving than He appears there. His wisdom may surpass ours beyond measure, but His goodness has already been revealed beyond doubt.

At last, when Job stood before God, all the speeches he had prepared fell silent. Not because his longing had been wrong, but because he had found what his heart had truly been seeking. In the light of God's presence, the need to defend himself disappeared. So it is with us. As we draw nearer to the Father, we discover that our greatest victory is not proving ourselves right, but knowing that we are held by the One who is always right and always good. His presence accomplishes what no explanation ever could—it transforms the heart until trust becomes its native language.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your presence become the answer my heart seeks.*
