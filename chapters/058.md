## 58 - Fear That Becomes Love

> *The fear of God is not the destination of the soul. It is the shadow that remains until we behold the Father's heart in the face of Christ.*

The soul that has not yet come to know the Father cannot help but fear Him. We fear what we do not understand, and we fear even more when we know there is something within us that is not as it should be. Such fear is not without purpose. It keeps us from settling comfortably into our brokenness. Until love awakens, fear may be the thread that still binds us to God, reminding us that we belong to One greater than ourselves.

Yet fear is never the Father's final word. He does not desire servants who obey because they tremble before His power. He desires children who trust His goodness. Little by little, as we discover that His righteousness is not against us but for us, terror begins to loosen its grip. The God we once imagined as our greatest threat is revealed to be our deepest refuge. We learn that the One who opposes our selfishness does so only because He longs to rescue our true life.

The fire of God does indeed burn, but it burns only what cannot live in His presence. It is not the soul He consumes but the falsehood that has wrapped itself around the soul. Pride, fear, resentment, and every form of death feel the heat of His love, while the life He planted within us finds warmth, freedom, and strength. What seemed at a distance like judgment becomes, when we draw near, the gentle fire of healing.

So if the thought of God still troubles you, do not flee from Him. Run toward Him. Come as you are, without disguise or defense. The Father has never desired your destruction; He has only desired your restoration. The very arms that seem fearful from afar are the arms that receive every returning child. His love does not wait until fear is gone. It is His love that casts fear out.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your perfect love cast out my fear.*
