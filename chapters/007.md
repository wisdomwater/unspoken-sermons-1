## 7 - The Courage to Seek

> *Faith is not content merely to repeat what it already knows. It longs to know the Father more deeply because love is never satisfied with distance.*

There are hearts that are content with what they have already learned, as though the words of yesterday could contain all the riches of the living God. But the child of God is not so. Having tasted the goodness of the Father, such a soul cannot help but ask, wonder, seek, and knock. New questions arise, not because faith is failing, but because faith is alive. The Father who awakens holy desire also delights to answer it.

The Scriptures are a precious gift, but they were never meant to be the end of our journey. They are the lamp that leads us to Christ, and Christ is the living revelation of the Father. In Him are hidden treasures that no book could exhaust. He is not silent toward those who seek Him. The same Lord who taught His first disciples still teaches every willing heart through His Spirit, unfolding truth as each soul becomes ready to receive it.

Do not be afraid, then, of questions born from love. There are questions that spring from pride and only wish to dispute, but there are others that rise from a heart that longs to know God more truly. Such questions are themselves a kind of prayer. The Father is not threatened by them. He planted that longing within you, and He is patient enough to lead you, step by step, into a deeper vision of His goodness.

The greatest danger is not that we ask too much of God, but that we expect too little. We settle for borrowed convictions when the Father invites us into living communion with His Son. He does not call us merely to repeat truths, but to behold them until they become life within us. Every fresh glimpse of Christ enlarges the heart, and every deeper knowledge of the Son reveals yet more of the inexhaustible love of the Father. Such seeking is not presumption. It is the trusting boldness of a child who knows there is always more goodness waiting to be discovered.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, lead me ever deeper into the living truth of Christ.*
