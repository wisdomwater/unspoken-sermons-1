## 72 - The Light We Walk In

> *We grow into truth not by possessing more answers, but by obeying the light we have already received. Every faithful step prepares the heart for greater light.*

The Father does not condemn His children because they once walked in darkness. He comes to them with light. The tragedy is never that we have been ignorant, but that we refuse the truth when it gently calls us forward. Christ is patient with misunderstanding, but He cannot lead the one who insists on remaining where he is. Every invitation of the Lord is an invitation into greater freedom, if only we will rise and follow.

Do not wait until every question has been answered before you obey. Understanding follows faithfulness. The soul learns the ways of God as the feet walk in them. The smallest act of obedience becomes another window through which the light of Christ enters the heart. Those who keep saying "yes" to the Lord discover that truths once hidden become quietly clear, not because they mastered them, but because they began to live them.

Nor let your faith be measured chiefly by the opinions you defend. The world has little need of louder arguments and much need of truer lives. When the love of Christ shapes our homes, our work, our friendships, our generosity, our honesty, and our mercy, the gospel becomes visible. A life quietly conformed to Jesus speaks more powerfully than the cleverest explanation ever could. Many are drawn to Christ first because they have seen His likeness reflected in one of His disciples.

Therefore, seek first the kingdom of God. Trust the Father enough to place His will above comfort, success, reputation, and security. The One who asks for everything does so because He intends to give everything in return. As His children become truthful, loving, and free, the world catches a glimpse of the Father Himself. And that living witness has always been the Church's greatest sermon.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, help me walk faithfully in the light You have already given.*
