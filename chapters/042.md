## 42 - Make Room for God

> *The soul becomes crowded not only with possessions, but with worries, ambitions, and restless thoughts. The Father gently empties our hearts so that He Himself may dwell there.*

How easily little things become great enough to hide God from us. A misplaced possession, a forgotten thought, an unpaid bill, an unreturned kindness, the opinions of others—none of these has the power to separate us from the Father. Yet if we allow them to fill our attention, they become like clouds that hide the sun. It is not that God has withdrawn, but that our hearts have become too crowded to notice His presence. The Father's desire is not merely to relieve our burdens, but to free us from whatever keeps us from resting in Him.

Do not think that what is small is therefore unimportant. The soul is shaped in the ordinary moments of life. One who is faithful in the smallest truth is already walking in the greater. Every forgotten object, every disappointment, every interrupted plan becomes an invitation to loosen our grasp on what cannot last. Even the truths we fear we have lost are safe with God. If they are truly His, they are not gone forever. What lives in Him can always be given again at the moment it is needed.

Many imagine that anxiety is the engine of faithful work, but fear has never produced the finest labor. The freest soul serves most faithfully because it works from love rather than from panic. Ambition, the hunger for praise, the desire to appear important, and the quiet worship of self all fill the house of the heart with clutter. God does not force His way into such a house. He waits with perfect patience, knocking until His child willingly opens the door. Love never invades; it is always welcomed.

If you find your heart too full to make room for Him, do not despair. Few of us are strong enough to cleanse our own souls. We discover that sweeping only raises more dust. Yet the Father has never asked you to purify yourself before coming to Him. He asks only that you keep turning toward Him in prayer. Every honest prayer opens the door a little wider, until at last the house that was crowded with fear becomes a home filled with His peace.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, clear my heart until there is room for You alone.*
