## 61 - The Courage to Come to the Father

> *The truest reverence is not afraid to draw near. Love does not honor the Father by standing at a distance, but by trusting Him enough to bring Him the whole truth of the heart.*

There are times when suffering strips away every careful word we have learned to say about God. We no longer know how to sound religious. We only know how to cry. Do not imagine that such honesty offends your Father. He is not searching for polished prayers but for true children. Job dared to bring his grief, his confusion, even his questions directly before God because he believed that, beneath all he could not understand, God was still just. That faith may look like complaint to others, but it is often the deepest form of trust.

There are those who think reverence means never speaking boldly to God. They keep Him at a respectful distance, as though awe were measured by silence and careful titles. Yet our Lord did not come to teach us how to flatter heaven. He came to reveal the Father. A child does not stand outside the door rehearsing compliments before entering home. He runs inside with scraped knees, tears, questions, and empty hands, certain that love will receive him before it explains him.

What confidence gave Job such freedom? It was this: he believed that if only he could stand before God, he would find not a tyrant, but One who would hear him. Though God seemed hidden, Job never ceased seeking His face. Even when he could not find God, he was never outside God's presence. Every anguished word entered the ears of the One who loved him. And the Father who seemed absent was already at work, refining him as gold is purified by fire—not to destroy him, but to reveal what His own hands had created.

There is a beautiful mystery at the heart of our faith. The Father has made you with needs that only He can satisfy, and in creating those needs He has also given you the right to bring them to Him. Your hunger for love, truth, forgiveness, peace, and life is no accident. It is His invitation. The God revealed in Jesus does not resent the cries of His children. Rather, He delights when they come expecting Him to be a Father. Every longing that leads you toward Him is already His work within you, and Christ Himself is the Father's answer to every deepest need of the human heart.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to trust Your heart and bring You every need.*
