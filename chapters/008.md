## 8 - The Faith That Trusts the Father

> *Faith is greatest not when it clings only to promises, but when it rests in the character of the One who makes them.*

Many souls are afraid to ask too much of God. They fear that hope itself may be presumption, and so they keep their expectations safely within the borders of what they believe has already been plainly promised. Yet a child does not measure a father's generosity by a list of guarantees. The child trusts because he knows the heart from which every gift comes. So our Father invites us to know Him, not merely His promises.

Bring everything, then, into His presence. Do not hide your questions, your desires, or your deepest longings. If what you seek is truly good, He delights to give it. And if what you ask would wound rather than heal, do not fear. Love is wiser than our desires. The Father will not grant what destroys His child, but neither will He despise the child for asking. He will answer with something better, patiently shaping the heart until it can rejoice in what is truly life.

The danger is not that we hope too much in God, but that we hope too little. We become cautious where love would make us bold. We imagine that God is guarding His dignity, when all the while He is opening His arms. We hesitate to draw near lest we ask beyond our place, yet the Son continually reveals a Father who delights in welcoming His children before they know how to speak wisely. His goodness is greater than our understanding, and His generosity reaches beyond our imagination.

Our Lord blessed those who believed without seeing because such faith rests not upon visible proof but upon the trustworthiness of God Himself. This is the higher faith. It believes that the Father's goodness extends farther than our sight, His mercy deeper than our fears, and His purposes beyond all we have yet imagined. Such faith does not invent its own god. It simply dares to believe that the Father revealed in Jesus is every bit as good as Jesus has shown Him to be—and better still than we have yet learned to hope.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to trust Your heart beyond what I can see.*
