## 4 - The Consuming Fire

> *The fire of God is not kindled against you, but for you. It burns only what keeps you from becoming all that love created you to be.*

Love can never be persuaded to desire anything less than what is truly good. It does not settle for appearances, nor does it leave the beloved imprisoned by all that diminishes life. Because God's love is perfect, it is unwavering. He seeks nothing less than the full beauty of His children—the likeness of Christ restored within them. Whatever hides that beauty, whatever distorts it or resists it, His love patiently sets itself against. This is why Scripture dares to say that our God is a consuming fire.

Do not imagine this fire as the fury of an offended ruler. It is the holy passion of a Father who refuses to abandon the work of His own hands. Fire destroys, but it also purifies. The goldsmith does not hate the gold because he places it in the furnace; he loves it too deeply to leave it mixed with what cannot endure. So the fire of God consumes only what has never truly belonged to you. Pride, fear, selfishness, falsehood—these are the things that tremble before His presence. The true self He has created can never be harmed by His love.

This is why the kingdom of God cannot be shaken. Everything built upon truth and love is eternal, while all that is false must eventually fall away. We often fear that surrendering to God will cost us ourselves, when in truth it is only the false self that has reason to fear. The nearer we come to Him, the more His fire becomes not a terror but a warmth within us. What once burned as correction begins to burn as life itself—the joyful presence of God filling every corner of the heart.

So do not flee from the fire. Flee with it. Let it drive you, not away from the Father, but into His embrace. There you will discover that holy fear is not the dread of punishment but the reverence that cannot bear to wound perfect love. And as His fire continues its quiet work within you, you will find yourself loving others more freely, for the heart that is purified by divine love cannot help but become a refuge for the world.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, burn away all that is not love.*
