## 77 - Knowing the Son

> *The goal of faith is not to possess perfect words about Christ, but to possess the living Spirit of Christ within us.*

The Lord did not come merely to leave us a book of flawless sentences. He came to awaken living hearts. Words, however holy, are always servants pointing beyond themselves. They become life only when the Spirit breathes through them into a willing soul. To cling to words while refusing the One to whom they point is to mistake the lamp for the sunrise.

Do not be troubled because you do not yet understand everything. The Father has never required perfect understanding before faithful obedience. Walk in the truth you already see. Practice the mercy you know. Forgive where you have been forgiven. Love where you have been loved. As you live the light you have received, greater light will quietly dawn. Truth unfolds itself to those who are willing to become true.

Nor should you fear humble mistakes as much as a proud certainty. A sincere heart that longs to obey Christ may misunderstand many things, yet it remains safely in His hands. The Lord is a patient Teacher. He gently removes our errors as we become able to bear greater truth. What hinders us most is not ignorance but the refusal to be taught.

To know the Son, then, is not simply to think rightly about Him. It is to receive His life. As His Spirit grows within us, we begin to hear the Father's voice—not with our physical ears, but with hearts awakened to love. We begin to recognize the Father's likeness because we see it shining in Jesus. And the more we behold Him, the more His own life becomes the life by which we live.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Lord Jesus, let Your Spirit teach my heart to know You truly.*
