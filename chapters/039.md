## 39 - The Tyranny of Things

> *You do not truly possess what you cannot peacefully surrender. Only the heart that rests in the Father is free to enjoy every gift.*

The tragedy of the rich young ruler was not simply that he loved his possessions. It was that he believed they were necessary to his well-being. The saddest part is not that he walked away, but that so many of us walk the same road without even his sorrow. We cling to what cannot satisfy us and call it wisdom. He at least felt the grief of turning from Life. Our danger is to feel nothing at all.

No created thing can become your home. Not wealth, not beauty, not reputation, not even the love of another soul. All these are good gifts, but none were made to carry the weight of your heart. Only the Father can do that. When He becomes your life, every created thing may be received with gratitude and held with freedom. You no longer possess them anxiously, but joyfully, as a child receives flowers from a loving hand, knowing the Giver is dearer than the gift.

For this reason, God is determined to free us from the tyranny of things. If we will not loosen our grip willingly, death itself will one day open our hands. Every possession, every title, every earthly advantage will fall away. Yet death is not the final remedy. It removes the objects we clung to, but it cannot by itself heal the heart that clung to them. The true work of redemption is deeper. God must teach the soul that life has never come from possessing, but that all true possession flows from sharing His own life.

This is why the loss of what we treasure may become the beginning of our freedom. When what once seemed essential is taken away, we may at last discover how completely it ruled us. The pain of that discovery is itself a mercy. A chain cannot be broken until it is first seen. The Father wounds only to heal, empties only to fill, and uncovers our slavery only because He means to make us His free children.

Nor is this danger reserved for the wealthy. One person is mastered by what he has; another by what he longs to have. Possession and craving are but two forms of the same bondage. The poor man who cannot be content without riches is no freer than the rich man who cannot bear to lose them. Freedom begins neither with abundance nor with poverty, but with a heart that has learned to say, "The Father is enough." Such a soul has already begun to inherit the kingdom that cannot be shaken.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, be my treasure and my freedom.*
