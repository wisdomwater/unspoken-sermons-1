## 88 - No Refuge from Love

> *The Father does not forgive by pretending sin does not matter. He forgives by refusing to surrender His child to the sin that is destroying them.*

There is something in us that takes satisfaction when wrongdoing is punished. Part of this comes from a true hatred of evil, but part comes from our own wounded pride. We do not merely hate the wrong; we begin to hate the wrongdoer. Then the suffering of another appears to us like justice, though often it is only revenge wearing a righteous name. But no such pleasure lives in God. He never delights in pain. He values suffering only where it can awaken, cleanse, and restore.

Because the Father forgives, He will not leave you untouched by the consequences of what is false in you. His forgiveness is not indifference. It is the fierce tenderness that refuses to hand a beloved child over to the selfishness that is killing them. You may flee from many things, but there is no refuge from the love of God. He will pursue every lie, every cruelty, and every hidden resistance—not because He despises you, but because He loves you too much to let sin become your final shape.

Jesus did not come merely to save you from the consequences of sin. He came to save you from sin itself—from the smallest desire to wound, deceive, possess, or exalt yourself above another. Salvation is entrance into the pure air of God’s own life, where the heart begins to see as He sees and love what He loves. The soul truly saved would rather endure any fire than carry one cherished falsehood into heaven. Christ does not cover an unchanged heart with borrowed goodness. He makes the heart itself good.

Be careful, then, of any teaching that makes the Father’s forgiveness depend upon the suffering of an innocent person in place of the guilty. Justice cannot be satisfied by injustice. Jesus did not come to persuade an unwilling God to forgive. He came from the Father because the Father had already determined to bring His children home. The cross does not reveal a Son protecting us from the Father; it reveals the Father and the Son united in one self-giving love, entering our darkness to overcome it from within.

You need not understand every mystery before you obey Christ. Indeed, no one understands Him merely by forming correct opinions. The mind of Christ grows in those who begin to do what He says. Follow Him in honesty, mercy, forgiveness, and love of neighbor, and He will teach you the Father. Truth will cast out falsehood as light drives darkness from a room. And at last you will discover that the heart of God was never demanding payment before it could love you. It was always seeking your willing return.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your love destroy every lie in me.*
