## 10 - The Forgiveness That Restores

> *God does not forgive by pretending evil never happened. He forgives by refusing to abandon the one who has done it until love has healed what sin has broken.*

When we hear difficult words from our Lord, we need not silence the deepest convictions He Himself has planted within us. If an interpretation makes Christ seem less loving, less truthful, or less beautiful than the One who welcomed children and forgave His enemies, then we have not yet seen its true meaning. The Spirit never asks us to believe what contradicts the character of the Son. Rather, He patiently leads us until every hard saying becomes another window into the Father's goodness.

The words of God are living words. They grow brighter as we grow nearer to Him. What seems severe from a distance often reveals itself, in the light of Christ, to be another expression of holy love. Truth is never divided against itself. The Father revealed in Jesus is the key to every mystery, and the heart that has learned to trust Him may wait without fear until fuller understanding comes.

So when we speak of forgiveness, we must not think of it as merely overlooking a wrong or setting aside a penalty. Human forgiveness often stops there. We release a debt while the wound remains untouched. But the Father's forgiveness reaches deeper. He cannot be content while anything still separates His child from Himself or from the life for which He was created. Divine forgiveness seeks restoration. It labors until truth is confessed, love is reborn, and what sin has torn apart is made whole again.

This is why God never gives up on those He forgives. His mercy is not forgetfulness but faithfulness. He follows His children into their darkness, not to condemn them, but to lead them back into the light. He is never satisfied with merely sparing us from consequences when He can restore us to communion. Such forgiveness is slower than indulgence, yet infinitely more tender. It is the forgiveness of a Father whose greatest joy is not simply to pardon His children, but to have them home again in His embrace.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, forgive me by making me whole in Your love.*
