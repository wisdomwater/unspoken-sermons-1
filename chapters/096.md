## 96 - The Righteousness of Faith

> *God does not pretend that we are righteous. He patiently makes us righteous. Faith is the first turning of the heart toward the Father who alone can complete His work within us.*

The righteousness God desires is never a mask laid over the soul but a new life growing within it. The Father does not delight in calling darkness light or evil good. He is truth itself. He does not save by pretending we are something we are not. He saves by drawing us, little by little, into the likeness of His own righteousness, until what He declares over us becomes what we truly are.

This is why faith is so precious. Faith is not merely agreeing that God exists. It is the whole heart turning toward Him in trust and obedience. When Abraham believed God, he placed his life into the Father's hands before he understood where that road would lead. Such faith is already the beginning of righteousness, for it yields itself to the One from whom all righteousness comes.

The soul that trusts God cannot remain unchanged. As a seed carries within itself the life of the tree, so faith carries within itself the life of holiness. A person who has truly turned toward the Father will begin to see more clearly, love more deeply, and recognize hidden selfishness that once passed unnoticed. This growth may be slow, and often humbling, but it is the sure work of divine life unfolding within the willing heart.

The Father asks no one to possess today a maturity they have not yet been given. He asks only that we continue walking toward the light we have received. Every fresh act of obedience enlarges our capacity to receive more. The child who keeps taking the Father's hand is already on the road to becoming like the Father.

So do not seek the comfort of being thought righteous while remaining unchanged. Seek instead the greater gift: to become righteous in truth. God's purpose is not merely to forgive your past but to restore His own image within you. He is content with nothing less than making His children share His own goodness, and He is patient enough to complete what His love has begun.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, make me truly righteous through trusting You.*
