## 43 - The Child Must Have the Father

> *Faith is not the certainty that we understand God's ways, but the quiet confidence that the Father understands ours. The deepest prayer of the soul is simply its longing to come home.*

Our Lord wondered whether, when He came again, He would find faith upon the earth. He did not ask whether He would find clever minds or busy hands, but trusting hearts. We are so easily persuaded by appearances. When heaven seems silent, we imagine the Father has forgotten us. Yet His seeming delay is never indifference. The One who holds all things in wisdom sees farther than we can imagine, and works toward an end so much greater than our present comfort that we often mistake His patience for absence. Faith is the child resting in the Father's love even when it cannot yet see what that love is preparing.

How much of our energy is spent trying to discover tomorrow instead of obeying today. We search for signs, explanations, and certainty, while Christ quietly calls us to faithfulness in the work before us. Obedience opens doors that speculation never can. The soul grows clear not by solving every mystery but by walking in the light already given. Every small act of trust teaches the heart to recognize the Father's voice more readily.

If you have ever felt unable to govern your own thoughts, conquer your fears, or become the person you long to be, do not imagine that such weakness separates you from God. Rather, it is often the beginning of true prayer. We discover that we cannot heal ourselves, explain ourselves, or even fully understand ourselves. Then we begin to look beyond ourselves toward the One from whom our life has come. The child who knows he cannot carry himself home has already taken the first step by crying out for his Father.

This longing for God is itself His gift. The ache for peace, the hunger for goodness, the desire to become whole—these are not signs that He is far away but that He is already drawing near. The Father who gave you life has not left you to wander alone within it. Every honest cry of the heart reaches Him before it reaches your lips. He knows you more deeply than you know yourself, and He delights to answer every prayer whose deepest desire is simply this: "Give me Yourself."

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, draw me ever deeper into Your presence.*
