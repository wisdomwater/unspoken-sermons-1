## 54 - Growing Into the Father's Likeness

> *To call God "Father" is the beginning of sonship; to love as He loves is its fulfillment. The Father's purpose is not merely to forgive His children, but to make them like His Son.*

The Father does not simply desire that you know you belong to Him. He desires that you grow into all that His children were created to become. Christ came not only to remove what separated us from the Father, but to lead us into His own relationship with the Father. Through Him we begin to see, to love, and to live with the very heart of God. This is the true meaning of becoming sons and daughters.

Such growth cannot be hurried. A child belongs to the family from birth, yet maturity comes only through years of living in the father's love. So it is with the soul. The first cry of "Abba, Father" is a glorious beginning, but it is only the beginning. The Father's life slowly spreads through every thought, desire, affection, and habit until there is no corner of the heart untouched by His goodness. Salvation is not merely a moment to remember but a new creation unfolding within us.

Nor is the Father's work finished with the soul alone. His purpose embraces the whole person. Even these frail bodies, burdened by weakness, weariness, and decay, are not forgotten. They too await redemption. The day will come when nothing in us will conceal the beauty God has been forming within. Our bodies themselves will become truthful expressions of the life they contain, as Christ's risen body perfectly revealed His living spirit. Then the likeness of the Father, now hidden in hope, will shine openly.

This hope reaches farther still. The whole creation waits for humanity to become what it was always meant to be. As we are restored into the likeness of the Son, the blessing of God flows outward into all He has made. The Father's love is never content with saving isolated souls; it seeks the renewal of the whole creation through sons and daughters who reflect His heart. Therefore, do not grow weary of the slow work of grace. Every step toward the Father's likeness is a step toward the healing of the world.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, make me like Your Son, within and without.*
