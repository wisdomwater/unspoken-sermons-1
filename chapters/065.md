## 65 - Seeing His Face

> *The soul's greatest longing is not to be proved right, but to behold the face of God. In His light, we lose ourselves only to discover the life we were created to live.*

Job stood before God expecting an answer, but he received something far greater. He saw the face of the Living One. In that holy presence, every argument faded away. The man who had longed to justify himself no longer desired it. He saw, perhaps for the first time, not merely the greatness of God, but the distance between perfect Love and all that still remained unhealed within himself. Yet this discovery was not condemnation. It was the beginning of true life. To see ourselves in the light of God is painful only because His love is so beautiful.

Do not fear the humility that comes from drawing near to the Father. There is a kind of self-contempt born of despair that crushes the soul, but there is another born from beholding divine goodness. This humility is a gift. As the dawn reveals every shadow without despising the landscape, so the presence of God uncovers all that is false within us, not to shame us away, but to call us deeper into His likeness. Only those who belong to Him can feel the holy ache of seeing how unlike Him they still are.

This is why suffering may become an unexpected mercy. Job's trials did not earn him the vision of God, nor were they proof of divine displeasure. They became the road by which the Father drew near. What once seemed only loss became the place where love revealed itself most clearly. So often we ask God to remove the darkness, while He longs to use even the darkness to bring us into a deeper knowledge of Himself. The greatest gift is never merely relief from pain, but communion with the One who is Life itself.

And when we have seen Him, we need not fear what lies ahead. Every sorrow surrendered to His will will one day blossom into joy. The Father's purpose has never been merely to spare us suffering, but to restore His own image within us. He offers us nothing less than Himself. Therefore, let us not cling to the old self that cannot inherit His kingdom. Let us deny all that separates us from His life, take up the cross that leads us home, and follow the Son. For beyond every cross waits the face our hearts have always been seeking, and in that face every tear will find its answer.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let me behold Your face and become like Your Son.*
