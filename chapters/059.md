## 59 - Fear Not, for I Am the Living One

> *The presence that first overwhelms us is the very presence that heals us. The glory of God is not the enemy of His children, but their eternal home.*

When John beheld the risen Lord in His unveiled glory, he fell to the ground as though life had left him. Yet the glory itself was not his danger. It was only that his heart had not yet learned to rest completely in the One he loved. So Jesus did not lessen His brightness or hide His majesty. Instead, He laid His hand upon His friend and spoke the words every fearful heart longs to hear: **"Fear not."**

Why should we not fear? Not because God is less powerful than we imagined, but because He is infinitely more good. The One whose face shines like the sun is the very One who gave Himself for us. The hands that hold all power are the same hands that touched the sick, lifted the fallen, and welcomed the weary. His glory is nothing other than the radiance of His love. The nearer we come to Him, the more we discover that what once seemed dreadful is our deepest safety.

We fear strength when we imagine strength without love. But in God, power and love are one. There is no force hidden within Him that opposes His children. His might exists only to sustain life, to destroy death, and to bring every wandering son and daughter home. The only thing His fire consumes is all that keeps us from becoming fully alive. Therefore the closer we draw to Him, the less fear remains, until awe itself is transformed into delight.

One day we shall no longer shrink from His glory. We shall rejoice in it as fish rejoice in the sea and children rejoice in their father's embrace. For the glory of God is not foreign to those made in His image. It is the very atmosphere for which we were created. The Living One surrounds us before and behind, the beginning and the end of our story, and His hand still rests upon every trembling heart, saying, "Fear not."

**Breath Prayer**

> *Living Lord, let Your perfect love quiet every fear.*
