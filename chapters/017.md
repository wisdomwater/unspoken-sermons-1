## 17 - Where Your Heart Lives

> *Your heart slowly becomes the home of whatever you treasure. Choose what cannot perish, and your soul will learn the life that never fades.*

Our Lord does not merely warn us against losing earthly treasures. He warns us against losing ourselves with them. Wherever we place our treasure, our heart quietly follows. If we fasten our deepest hopes to what fades, then our own souls begin to share in that fading. The tragedy is not that earthly things pass away. It is that an eternal heart was never created to make its home among passing things.

The Father has made within you a place that nothing temporary can satisfy. It was formed for Himself. Yet we often fill it with praise from others, success, possessions, pleasures, or the desire to be admired. None of these things are evil in themselves, but they cannot bear the weight of the soul. What is meant to be a passing gift becomes a master, and the heart begins to wear the same decay as the treasure it clings to.

This corruption is often invisible at first. A life may appear prosperous while the inner person grows restless, anxious, and hollow. The Father sees what even we may not see. He looks beyond appearances into the secret chamber of the heart, not to condemn us, but because He longs to rescue us from fastening our lives to what cannot last. His love calls us away from every lesser treasure toward the only treasure that cannot be stolen, spoiled, or consumed.

To treasure God is not to despise the world He has made. It is to enjoy every good gift without asking it to become your life. When the Father Himself becomes your treasure, everything else finds its proper place. The heart grows lighter, freer, and more whole, because it has finally come home. Nothing that moth or rust can touch has power to wound the soul whose deepest riches are hidden in the everlasting love of God.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, become the treasure of my heart.*
