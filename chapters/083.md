## 83 - The Freedom of the Children

> *God does not desire your obedience as a master desires service. He desires it as a Father who will not rest until His child is free enough to love what He loves.*

There is a kind of obedience that is still slavery. A person may do what is right because punishment frightens him, because duty presses heavily upon him, or because he hopes to earn the favor of God. Such obedience may be better than rebellion, but it is not yet the freedom of the children. The Father does not wish to keep servants in His house. He is raising sons and daughters who will one day share His heart, delight in His goodness, and choose His will because they have discovered it to be life itself.

You may still feel the servant within you. You may obey reluctantly, complain beneath the weight of life, or secretly wish that God would leave you to yourself. Do not therefore conclude that you are no child of His. The true child is not the one in whom every trace of slavery has vanished, but the one who has begun to oppose it. When you turn toward the Father and say, “Make me willing to want what You want,” the chains are already loosening. You have taken the side of God against all that keeps you from Him.

We often imagine freedom to mean independence: the right to follow every impulse and call it our own. Yet much that rises within us does not come from our deepest self at all. Fear speaks. Appetite speaks. Old wounds, inherited habits, wounded pride, and restless desires all speak at once, each claiming to be our true voice. To obey them without question is not freedom. It is to be ruled by masters whose names we scarcely know. The self that insists upon having its own way may be the least free self of all.

True freedom is found when the life within you comes into harmony with the Life from which you came. A branch does not become free by cutting itself from the tree. A river does not become free by fleeing its source. You become free by yielding yourself to the creative love that knows the truth of your being and is still laboring to bring it forth. The Father is nearer to you than you are to yourself. His will is not a foreign will imposed upon you, but the loving purpose from which your truest self was born.

This is the freedom Jesus gives. He does not merely break the chains around you; He awakens the child within you. He reveals a Father so good that surrender becomes homecoming, and obedience becomes participation in the divine life. The Father will not leave you forever in the house as a servant, whether fearful or rebellious. He will teach you, correct you, and, when necessary, let His holy fire consume the lies that make you afraid of Him. His purpose is not to master you, but to make you free indeed—free to love, free to trust, and free to share His joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, free me to live as Your child.*
