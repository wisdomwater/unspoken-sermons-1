## 73 - Walk in the Light You Have

> *God asks you to be faithful to the light you have today. As you walk in it, He will graciously reveal the light you need tomorrow.*

Do not be anxious that your understanding is incomplete. The Father has never required His children to possess perfect knowledge before they begin the journey. He asks only that they walk faithfully in what they already know. Every honest act of obedience prepares the soul for a deeper vision of truth. As we follow Christ, He Himself becomes our teacher, gently correcting what is false and enlarging what is true.

Do not seek first to win arguments or persuade others of your opinions. Seek instead to become the kind of person whose life makes Christ believable. A loving heart, a truthful tongue, generous hands, and quiet faithfulness speak with a power that no system of theology can equal. When people see the life of Jesus reflected in His disciples, they are drawn, not merely to our conclusions, but to the living Lord Himself.

Take courage also in the steadfast love of the Father. He does not abandon His children to the prisons they build for themselves. His purpose has always been to bring every wandering heart home. Even when we resist Him, His love continues its patient labor, using conviction, discipline, and mercy to awaken us. The Good Shepherd does not cease seeking until His lost sheep are found. His judgments are never the surrender of love but its faithful work of restoration.

Therefore, let us walk in the light we have already received. Do the good you know to do. Leave behind what your conscience has already recognized as false. Trust the Father for the next step, and then the next. As we continue in this humble obedience, truth and love grow together within us until, at last, God and His children rejoice in perfect communion. This is the end toward which all His work has always moved: that He may be all in all, and that His children may find their everlasting home in the joy of His heart.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, help me faithfully walk in the light You have given until I dwell fully in Yours.*
