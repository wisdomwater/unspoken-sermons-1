## 48 - Pray, and Do Not Lose Heart

> *Prayer is not precious merely because it changes circumstances, but because it draws the soul into communion with the Father. He begins answering long before we can see the answer.*

Some speak of prayer as though its only value were the peace it brings the one who prays. Yet prayer is far more than a comforting habit. It is the meeting of the child with the living Father. Even when the answer we seek has not yet appeared, every sincere prayer opens the heart a little wider to His presence. To speak with God is already to receive something greater than many of the gifts for which we ask.

There are moments when words flow freely, and there are moments when longing is too deep for speech. Sometimes the truest prayer is nothing more than a silent turning of the soul toward God. In that holy communion we discover that our greatest need has never been merely the answer to our requests, but the Father Himself. The one who seeks Him above all else will find that even unanswered desires become pathways into deeper fellowship.

Do not mistake delay for neglect. The Father wastes no time, though His work often unfolds beyond our sight. A seed hidden beneath the soil has already begun to grow long before green shoots appear above the earth. So God may be answering your prayer in places within you that your own awareness has not yet reached. He is preparing the heart to receive what it has asked for, shaping the gift and the receiver together. What seems like waiting may be the quiet beginning of the answer itself.

Nor should we fear the Father's justice. His vengeance is unlike ours. He does not delight in pain or retaliation but in destroying everything that destroys His children. The enemies He most longs to overcome are the pride, fear, bitterness, and falsehood that imprison the soul. When you find yourself battling these hidden adversaries, do not lose heart. The struggle itself is evidence that the true self is awakening. Keep praying. Keep asking. The Father is already at work, and He will not cease until every enemy to love has been overcome and His child stands free.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me to trust Your hidden work within me.*
