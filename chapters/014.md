## 14 - The Name the Father Knows

> *God does not merely know your name; He knows the person you were created to become. His deepest delight is to reveal you to yourself through His love.*

Much of what we call identity is given by the world. We are known by our work, our history, our successes, our failures, or the names others have spoken over us. Yet these are but labels upon the surface. The Father sees more deeply. Before a single word was spoken about you by another, He knew the true person hidden within His own loving purpose.

This is why Scripture speaks of a new name. It is not merely another title, but the Father's own knowledge of His child. It is His perfect understanding of who you truly are—untouched by your fears, unmarred by your sins, and undiminished by the years you have wandered. Hidden within the heart of God is the person He has always seen, and toward that person His love has been quietly working from the beginning.

The promise of the white stone is therefore wonderfully personal. The new name is known only to the one who receives it because no one else has lived your story, nor has anyone else been called in quite the same way. God's love is never general or distant. As sunlight falls upon every leaf while giving each its own brightness, so the Father knows each of His children with an intimacy that can never be borrowed or shared. His call to you is unlike His call to any other soul.

To overcome, then, is not to become someone else. It is to let every false name fall away until only the Father's word remains. One day you shall hear what He has always thought of you. In that moment there will be no striving left, no need to prove yourself, no fear that you have been misunderstood. You will know yourself because you will know yourself as He has always known you. And that knowledge will not diminish your wonder—it will be your everlasting joy.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, teach me the name You have always spoken over me.*
