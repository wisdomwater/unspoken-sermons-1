## 45 - The Gift Behind Every Gift

> *Every good gift is meant to lead us to the Giver. The Father's greatest answer to prayer is always more of Himself.*

The Father delights to give, yet His gifts are never meant to end with themselves. Every blessing carries within it an invitation to know the heart from which it came. A gift received without recognizing the Giver has not yet fulfilled its purpose. God does not simply satisfy our needs; He uses our needs to draw us into communion with Himself. When we learn to see His love in every gift, we begin to discover that every good thing has always been pointing beyond itself to Him.

Do not be afraid to bring every desire before your Father. Some imagine that true spirituality means asking for nothing, leaving every wish unspoken lest it somehow displease God. But this is not the confidence of a child. A loving child tells his father everything—the smallest hope, the deepest fear, the simplest longing. As each desire is laid before God, it is gently purified in His presence. What is unworthy begins to lose its hold, and what is good grows clearer in the light of His love.

Our Lord Himself showed us this holy freedom. In Gethsemane He spoke honestly of His longing that the cup might pass from Him, yet He rested more deeply in the Father's will than in His own desire. This is not resignation but perfect trust. Love hides nothing from the Father, yet it trusts the Father's wisdom above its own understanding. Such prayer does not attempt to persuade God to become loving; it rests in the certainty that He already is.

Never imagine that prayer must overcome an unwilling God. The Father is not reluctant to bless His children. He does not wait to be persuaded but longs to give what is truly good. Therefore pray without fear, and do not lose heart if the answer comes in a different form than you expected. His refusals are never the refusals of indifference, but the careful choices of perfect love. Whatever He withholds, He withholds only to make room for something better, until at last every gift finds its fulfillment in the greatest gift of all—His own life shared with His children.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let every gift lead me more deeply into You.*
