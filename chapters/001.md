I absolutely love this.

And I think I've discovered something that should become the defining characteristic of this project.

Notice what MacDonald says near the beginning:

> *"It is not for the sake of setting forth this lesson that I write... but for the sake of a truth, a revelation about God..."*

That's MacDonald.

He begins with a familiar text, but almost immediately asks:

> **"What does this tell us about God?"**

Not,

> "What should I do?"

Not,

> "How should I behave?"

But,

> **"Who is the Father?"**

Everything else follows from that revelation.

I think our devotionals should preserve that movement.

## 1 — Becoming a Child

> *God does not ask you to become less than yourself. He invites you to become what you were before fear taught you otherwise.*


When our Lord placed a child among His disciples, He was not praising innocence as though children had never done wrong. Neither was He suggesting that childhood itself is perfection. He was revealing something far deeper. He was saying, "Look carefully. Here is a picture of the kind of heart that belongs in My Father's kingdom."

The child trusts before he understands. He receives before he possesses. He delights before he calculates. Such a heart has not yet learned that greatness is measured by being above another. It knows only the joy of being loved.

Do not think, however, that God loves only those whose childlikeness is easy to see. Many have lived beneath burdens that have hidden it. Fear, shame, neglect, and sin may cover the child beneath layers of self-protection until even they have forgotten who they are. Yet the Father has not forgotten. He sees beyond every wound to the child He first imagined. His work is never merely to forgive your failures, but to uncover the beloved son or daughter buried beneath them.

This is why Christ embraced a child. He was showing us not only what we must become, but what God Himself never ceases to see. The Father does not look first at what you have become. He looks at who you were created to be. His love is patient enough to remove everything that hides His image until your true life shines again.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, restore the child You created me to be.*
