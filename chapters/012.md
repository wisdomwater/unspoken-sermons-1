## 12 - The Heart That Refuses Love

> *God never stops loving us. What closes the door is not His unwillingness to forgive, but our unwillingness to receive the love that would make us new.*

There are states of the heart that make forgiveness seem impossible—not because the Father's love has failed, but because we refuse to let it enter. The sun may shine with all its strength, yet a house with every shutter fastened remains dark within. God's love is always reaching toward His children, but love cannot restore where every entrance has been deliberately closed.

One such door is shut when we refuse to forgive another. To cling to resentment is to keep alive the very thing that God's mercy longs to destroy. Hatred promises justice, yet it quietly imprisons the one who carries it. The Father cannot simply bless such a condition, for His desire is not to leave us in our bitterness but to free us from it. His refusal is itself an act of mercy, refusing to call health what is truly sickness.

When we begin—even tremblingly—to turn toward the one we have rejected, something remarkable happens. We find that God's own forgiveness has been waiting for us there. As our hearts open to another, they become able to receive the Father's embrace. We discover that His command to forgive was never an impossible burden laid upon us, but an invitation into the freedom He Himself has always desired for us.

Never imagine that God delights in leaving anyone beyond hope. Such a thought mistakes the Father's heart entirely. He does not abandon His children because they have wandered far. He labors without ceasing until every falsehood, every hatred, and every fear has lost its hold. Sin is unpardonable only in this sense: it cannot remain. It must be burned away. The Father will not make peace with the darkness because He loves His children too well to leave them in it. His forgiveness is the holy fire that will not rest until all that is unlike love has given way to love itself.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, open my heart to receive and to give Your forgiving love.*
