## 24 - My God in the Darkness

> *Faith is not proven when God feels near, but when the heart still whispers, "My God," even in the deepest night. Love remains faithful when every feeling has disappeared.*

When our Lord cried, "My God, my God, why have You forsaken me?" He was not abandoning the Father. He was clinging to Him with the last strength of His soul. Every comfort had vanished. Every sense of the Father's nearness had been withdrawn. Yet from the depths of that unfathomable darkness came those two words: *My God.* Even when He could no longer feel the Father's presence, He refused to let go of the Father's goodness.

There are seasons when the heavens seem silent. Prayer feels empty, hope grows dim, and the heart wonders where God has gone. We are often tempted to believe that His hiddenness means His absence. But the cross reveals something far more wonderful. The Father may be unseen, yet He has not departed. Indeed, He is often nearest when we can no longer perceive Him. Love does not cease because our senses fail to find it.

In that terrible hour, Christ embraced the whole of our human experience. He entered the place where every child of God has feared to walk—the place where faith has no feeling to sustain it. Yet He did not surrender to despair. His cry was not the cry of unbelief but of unwavering trust. The darkness could surround Him, but it could not separate Him from the One He still called *My God*. In that simple confession, faith achieved its greatest victory.

If one day your own heart can say nothing more than those same words, do not think you have failed. Perhaps you have come nearer to the faith of Christ than you know. The Father does not ask you to manufacture peace or certainty. He asks only that you continue to belong to Him. Hold fast, however weakly. Beneath every darkness, His everlasting love is holding you more securely than you are holding Him.

**Breath Prayer**

> *My God, hold me fast when I cannot feel Your presence.*
