## 95 - No One Is Lost

> *Every departure from our arms is an arrival into other arms. The Father is never taking one child into darkness, but receiving that child into a greater light.*

When Lazarus died a second time, Martha and Mary surely grieved, but they could not grieve as before. They knew now that the grave had not swallowed him. They had seen him return from beyond their sight. They knew that death had not destroyed the man they loved, and that the Lord who had called him once from the tomb would keep him safely until all were gathered home.

You may say that no such miracle has been given to you. Yet the raising of Lazarus was not meant for his sisters alone. It was given as a sign for every sorrowing heart. The story stands before you so that, when someone you love passes from this room into the next, you may know that the unseen is not empty. The Father has not ceased to hold them merely because you can no longer touch them.

What appears to us as loss is, from another shore, reunion. While one pair of arms is left empty, other arms may be opening in joy. We see only the farewell; God sees the welcome also. He is continually receiving those whom we call taken away, and nothing in His care is ever misplaced, forgotten, or diminished.

This does not mean that you must not weep. Love feels absence, and the heart may ache because the beloved is no longer near in the old familiar way. But sorrow need not become despair. Your tears may fall within trust. You may miss the voice, the face, the daily presence, while still believing that the person you love is more alive than you can presently understand.

The deepest comfort is not merely the hope of seeing one another again. It is God Himself. Without Him, even endless companionship could not satisfy the soul. In Him, no love is wasted, no bond is broken, and no child is lost. He is preparing both the departed and those who remain for a life in which love will be purified, enlarged, and made eternal.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, hold those I love until we meet again.*
