## 16 - Alone with the Father

> *The soul that rests in the Father's delight no longer needs the world's applause. To be fully known and fully loved by God is enough.*

Each glimpse of your true self is a gift from the Father. As His life grows within you, the false names you have carried begin to fade, and the quiet radiance of the new name grows clearer. This is not the discovery of your own greatness, but of God's faithful craftsmanship. You begin to see yourself less through your own anxious thoughts and more through the loving thought He has always held of you.

There is no room for pride here. Pride compares. Love simply becomes. The rose has no desire to be the cedar, nor does the eagle envy the sparrow. Each fulfills the joy of its Maker by becoming exactly what it was created to be. So it is with every child of God. Your calling is not to surpass another soul but to answer the unique invitation the Father has spoken over your own life.

And because every person bears a different revelation of God, we need one another with holy reverence. There is a conversation between your brother and the Father that you can never overhear, just as there is one between you and the Father that belongs to no one else. When we honor this mystery, we cease trying to make others into copies of ourselves. Instead, we rejoice that each life reveals another facet of the inexhaustible beauty of God.

What peace there is in such a life. You no longer ask whether you are greater or lesser than another. You ask only whether you are becoming more truly the person the Father is making. His delight becomes enough. His approval quiets every restless ambition. And if you stumble, His refining fire remains—not to cast you away, but to finish the work He began. For His purpose has never changed: that one day you should stand before Him, fully yourself, fully His, and radiant with the name He has always known.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, let Your delight become enough for me.*
