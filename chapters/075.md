## 75 - The Life That Becomes Our Light

> *Christ came not merely to tell us about life, but to share His own life with us. As we behold the Son, we learn to live as children of the Father.*

The life that filled Jesus was not meant to remain His alone. The Son chose, with perfect freedom and perfect joy, to live wholly for the Father. That willing surrender became the pattern and the power of our own salvation. We, too, were created to become sons and daughters, not merely by birth, but by freely saying "yes" to the Father's love. Our deepest happiness is found when our will rests within His.

This is why Christ is called the Light of the world. Light does more than reveal; it awakens desire. In Jesus we see a life more beautiful than anything we could have imagined. His gentleness, courage, purity, forgiveness, and unwavering trust stir something already planted within us. The life of God, hidden like a seed in every human heart, begins to long for its true fulfillment. We behold the Son, and our own hearts whisper, "This is what I was made for."

Never imagine that the Son came to persuade us of a reluctant Father. Everything Jesus is reveals what the Father has always been. The compassion of Christ is the compassion of the Father. The patience of Christ is the patience of the Father. The self-giving love that carried Jesus to the cross is the eternal love that has forever burned within the heart of God. The Son does not soften the Father's heart toward us—He unveils it.

At the center of all creation stands this everlasting communion of love between the Father and the Son. From that divine fellowship the universe was born, and toward that fellowship all creation is being drawn. Christ invites us into His own relationship with the Father, saying in effect, "Come home with Me. Share My joy. Live by the same love that lives in Me." This is eternal life—not merely life without end, but life filled with the very love that has always existed in God. As we receive that love and return it freely, the light of Christ becomes our own, and we discover that we have finally come home.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Father, draw me into the life and love You share with Your Son.*
