## 84 - Free Indeed

> *The Son does not free you merely by removing your chains. He frees you by awakening within you the heart of a child.*

You may ask Christ to free you, yet still imagine freedom as escape from pain, hardship, or restraint. But He came to free you from something deeper: the false self that clings to its own will, seeks its own honor, and fears surrender to the Father. That self is the true prison. So long as you must defend it, satisfy it, and preserve its little kingdom, you remain bound, even while calling yourself free.

The Son sets you free by making you like Himself. He teaches you to turn toward the Father with trust, obedience, and joy. He does not offer you a secret scheme by which transformation may happen without your consent. He calls you to follow. When you ask, “Lord, what would You have me do?” and then begin to do the truth already shown to you, He awakens within you the heart the Father placed there from the beginning.

Christ did not die merely to save you from suffering. He died to save you from being false, unjust, selfish, and divided within yourself. He died that you might live as He lives—by giving yourself wholly to the Father. The way into life is therefore a kind of death: the surrender of the self that insists on being its own source, its own master, and its own reward. Yet what dies is not your true humanity. It is only that which has kept your humanity from becoming whole.

Look, then, at Jesus. His freedom is His perfect confidence in the Father. He fears nothing but evil, desires nothing apart from the Father’s will, and rests in the certainty that the Father is entirely good. He reveals God not only through mighty works or compassionate words, but through the joy of His obedience. In the Son’s glad surrender, we see what the Father is like: One worthy of absolute trust, infinite expectation, and undivided love.

This is the freedom prepared for you. To become a child of the Father is to stand at the heart of reality, where nothing true can enslave you. You may serve everyone, yet belong to no master but Love. You may lose comfort, reputation, possessions, even life itself, and still remain free. For the one who is united with the Father cannot be imprisoned by anything in the universe. Such a person is free indeed.

**Breath Prayer**

> *Jesus, awaken in me the freedom of a child.*
